**Updated June 2026** — added Truthear Pure (the new Listener-tuned $90 hybrid that's reshaping the sub-$100 conversation).

The sub-$100 IEM market is the most competitive segment in audio. Chinese brands have spent the last five years undercutting Sennheiser, Sony, and Audio-Technica on the same tier — and they're now better than the legacy brands at this price.

This guide is the **11 picks we'd actually buy** in 2026, sorted by price. Each entry includes the spec, the sound profile, who it's for, and what to skip it for. No filler. No "honorable mentions". 11 picks, real recommendations.

## TL;DR — top picks by budget

- **Under $25**: 7Hz Salnotes Zero ($25) or Moondrop Chu 2 ($20)
- **$40 sweet spot**: Simgot EW200 ($40)
- **$60-80 sweet spot**: Truthear Hexa ($79) — the technical winner
- **Best $90 buys**: **Truthear Pure** ($89) — warmest musical pick / **Moondrop Aria 2** ($89) — single-DD reference
- **For planar fans under $100**: Skip this range; save for Letshuoer S12 Pro at $160 instead

## How we picked

Three criteria:
1. **Sound** — tuning targets a reasonable curve (Harman / VDSF / similar), no offensive peaks, no obvious dips
2. **Build** — better-than-price-tag construction, detachable cable, no flimsy hinges or loose nozzles
3. **Community sentiment** — sustained positive reception on Head-Fi, r/iems, ASR (not just launch hype)

We excluded: limited-edition/imported-only stock, IEMs with known QC issues at launch, IEMs older than 18 months that have been clearly surpassed.

## #1 — Moondrop Chu 2 — $20

**Single 10mm dynamic, 28Ω, 120dB, fixed cable**

The Chu 2 is the IEM that broke the price-to-performance curve. At $20, it ships with Moondrop's VDSF tuning (essentially a polite Harman target), a single 10mm DD, and a fixed but well-braided cable. Sound is balanced, vocal-forward, surprisingly clean for the price.

- **Who it's for**: Anyone wanting their first "real" audiophile IEM, gift buyers, second pair to keep in a bag
- **Skip if**: You want detachable cable (Chu 2's is fixed)

[Buy at Linsoul](https://www.linsoul.com/products/moondrop-chu-ii) · [Buy at HiFiGo](https://hifigo.com/)

## #2 — 7Hz Salnotes Zero — $25

**Single 10mm dynamic, 32Ω, 108dB, detachable 2-pin**

The original Zero set a new floor for $25 IEMs in 2023. Two years later it's still on every "first IEM" list because the tuning is genuinely good — slightly warmer than the Chu 2, with more sub-bass presence. The shell is plain but the **2-pin detachable cable** is a real upgrade over the Chu's fixed cable.

- **Who it's for**: First IEM buyers who want cable flexibility, bass-light listeners
- **Skip if**: You want the absolute sharpest treble (Salnotes Zero is polite)

[Buy at Linsoul](https://www.linsoul.com/products/7hz-salnotes-zero) · [Buy at HiFiGo](https://hifigo.com/)

## #3 — KZ Castor (Harman Version) — $35

**Dual 10mm dynamic, tunable bass switch, 32Ω, detachable 2-pin**

KZ has a reputation for V-shaped, treble-spiky IEMs — the Castor is the brand's correction. Comes in two versions; **get the Harman tuning, not the Bass tuning** (Bass version is too dark for most). The on-shell switch lets you toggle a small bass boost.

Build is plastic but solid; KZ has always nailed ergonomics.

- **Who it's for**: People who want bass adjustability, KZ fans who liked their previous offerings, dual-DD enthusiasts
- **Skip if**: You don't trust KZ's QC (some bad runs in the past — 2024-25 production has been fine)

[Buy at Linsoul](https://www.linsoul.com/products/kz-castor) · [Buy at HiFiGo](https://hifigo.com/)

## #4 — Simgot EW200 — $40

**Single 10mm dynamic, dual-cavity DLC diaphragm, 16Ω, 126dB**

The EW200 is the **technical champion under $50**. Simgot's DLC (diamond-like carbon) diaphragm delivers detail and speed that punches into $100 territory. Tuning is more energetic than the Salnotes Zero — slightly U-shaped, with sparkly treble.

The metal shell is gorgeous for $40. Stock cable is acceptable. Easy to drive (126 dB sensitivity).

- **Who it's for**: Anyone wanting "the best IEM under $50", treble-detail lovers, EDM/pop listeners
- **Skip if**: You're treble-sensitive (EW200 has presence-region energy)

[Buy at Linsoul](https://www.linsoul.com/products/simgot-ew200) · [Buy at HiFiGo](https://hifigo.com/)

## #5 — Truthear Zero Red — $55

**Dual 10mm dynamic (1 DD + 1 passive radiator), 14.8Ω, 117dB**

The Zero Red is **the bass-head pick at this price**. Truthear paired an active 10mm DD with a passive radiator to deliver sub-bass slam that hybrids can't match. Mid-bass is also elevated, giving rock and EDM real weight.

Treble is more polite than the EW200 — listenable for hours. Build is resin shell, lightweight, comfortable.

- **Who it's for**: Bass-heads, EDM/hip-hop fans, rock listeners
- **Skip if**: You listen to classical or want neutral tuning (Zero Blue is the neutral version)

[Buy at Linsoul](https://www.linsoul.com/products/truthear-zero-red) · [Buy at HiFiGo](https://hifigo.com/)

## #6 — 7Hz Sonus — $60

**1DD + 1BA hybrid, 20Ω, 105dB**

7Hz Sonus is the **dark horse pick** in this list. It's a 1DD+1BA hybrid that pulls off a smooth, neutral-warm tuning with no offensive peaks. Imaging and stage are wider than its driver count suggests.

Build is metal-faceplate-on-resin, comfortable. Stock cable is plain but functional.

- **Who it's for**: Long-session listeners, jazz/acoustic fans, anyone tired of V-shaped IEMs
- **Skip if**: You want excitement or sub-bass slam (Sonus is calm by design)

## #8 — Truthear Hexa — $79

**1DD + 3BA tribrid (10mm LCP DD + 2 composite BA + 1 high BA), 20.5Ω, 120dB**

The **technical benchmark under $100**. The Hexa packs four drivers into a $79 IEM and tunes them to a Harman-derivative target. Detail retrieval, soundstage, imaging, and treble extension all exceed what you expect at the price.

Trade-off: needs a clean source to shine (phone DAC is OK but not optimal), and the brighter treble isn't for everyone.

- **Who it's for**: Technical-detail-focused listeners, classical / jazz / orchestral, anyone with a dongle DAC or DAP

## Buying tips

**Tip 1: Tips matter more than you think.** Most ChiFi IEMs ship with mediocre silicone tips. Budget $10-15 for **Spinfit CP145** or **Moondrop Spring Tips** — the seal improvement is night-and-day for bass response and isolation.

**Tip 2: Source matters at $80+.** Below $50, your phone is fine. Above $80, a $9 Apple USB-C dongle or $30 Moondrop Dawn Pro will noticeably improve the experience.

**Tip 3: Returns are easy at major retailers.** [Linsoul](https://www.linsoul.com), [HiFiGo](https://hifigo.com), and [Apos Audio](https://apos.audio) all have 30-day return windows. Don't agonize — order, listen, return if not for you.

**Tip 4: New product cycle is fast.** ChiFi brands ship new IEMs every 2-3 months. The list above is current as of mid-2026; check our [Reviews](/reviews/) for what's launched since.
