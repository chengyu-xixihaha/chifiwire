## Closed-back vs open-back — which to buy?

**Buy closed-back** if:
- You commute or work in an open office
- You record / mix audio (need isolation from leak / monitor speakers)
- You live with others who don't want to hear your music
- You travel and need ANC variants

**Buy open-back** if:
- You listen at home alone with no isolation needs
- You want maximum soundstage and air
- You value timbre and naturalness above isolation
- You don't move while listening

For most listeners, **both is the right answer** — closed for context-aware use, open for "real" home listening. See our [open-back guide]({{< ref "guides/best-chinese-open-back-headphones-under-500" >}}).

## Source pairing

Most of these headphones are **planar magnetic** and need:
- **A real desktop amp** (Topping A30 Pro, SMSL HO200, etc.) for full performance
- **A high-current dongle DAC** (FiiO KA17, Questyle M15i) for portable
- **Not a phone alone** — planar headphones at 30-50Ω + low sensitivity = phone underpowered

For the wired HE-R10D, HE400se Closed, Cosmo, Eternal: budget $100-200 for a source.
For the wireless STAX SPIRIT S5: no source needed — Bluetooth is the source.

## Buying tips

**Tip 1: Closed-back isolation is real but not full.** Don't expect ANC-tier isolation. Closed-back attenuates 10-20 dB, ANC attenuates 25-35 dB.

**Tip 2: Pad swaps significantly change sound.** Brainwavz, Dekoni, and aftermarket pads change comfort and tonality. Budget $30-60 for pad experimentation.

**Tip 3: Closed-back is heavier than open-back.** A 350g closed-back feels significantly heavier than a 350g open-back due to weight distribution.

**Tip 4: Wireless audio is lossy.** Even with LDAC, wireless audio is compressed. Wired is always better for fidelity.

**Tip 5: ANC is good in 2026 — but still not perfect.** ANC handles low-frequency engine noise; doesn't handle voices or higher-frequency office sounds. Don't expect silence in a busy cafe.

## Common mistakes

- **Buying ANC for audiophile use** — ANC adds artifacts; pure passive closed-back sounds better
- **Buying closed-back for soundstage** — closed = small soundstage by definition; if you want big stage, go open-back
- **Underpowering** — closed-back planars need real amps to perform; don't pair with phones
