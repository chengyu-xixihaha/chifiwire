## Why these 4 combos?

These aren't random. Each represents a **different sonic direction** that defines audiophile preferences:

- **Combo A (Detail-focused)** = analytical, neutral, "reference"
- **Combo B (Warm)** = lush, vocal-led, musical
- **Combo C (Casual)** = no commitment to source upgrade
- **Combo D (Power)** = planar speed, electronic / metal optimized

Most listeners' **first real audiophile IEM determines their hearing direction for years**. Pick the combo that matches your music, not the one with the best specs.

## Combo A — Detail Setup ($119)

### [Truthear Hexa]({{< ref "reviews/truthear-hexa-review" >}}) ($79) + Fosi DS2 dongle DAC ($40)

**Why this combo**: The Hexa is the gold-standard sub-$100 hybrid for detail and resolution. The DS2 dongle delivers genuine power and quality with balanced 4.4mm output for future upgrades.

**Sound character**: Analytical, neutral-leaning, slightly bright. Reveals everything in the recording. Won't flatter bad recordings.

**Best for**:
- Music: prog rock, classical, electronic, complex arrangements
- Use: home listening, focused listening sessions
- Personality: curious, willing to hear flaws as well as virtues

**Less ideal for**:
- Music: vocal-heavy pop, R&B, ballads
- Use: background music at low volume (loses energy)
- Personality: warm-tonality preference

**Upgrade path**: Hexa → [Nova]({{< ref "reviews/truthear-nova-review" >}}) ($150) → [S15]({{< ref "reviews/letshuoer-s15-review" >}}) ($329)

## Combo B — Warm Setup ($99)

### [Truthear Pure]({{< ref "reviews/truthear-pure-review" >}}) ($90) + Apple USB-C dongle ($9)

**Why this combo**: The Pure is the warm-tuned counterpart to the Hexa. Same brand engineering quality, Listener tuning makes it musically engaging. The Apple dongle is good enough to drive it well; you can upgrade later.

**Sound character**: Warm, smooth, vocal-presence-forward. Forgives bad recordings. Sounds "musical" rather than "analytical".

**Best for**:
- Music: jazz, R&B, pop, vocal-led genres, ballads
- Use: long listening sessions, background music, dinner-party listening
- Personality: relaxed, music-as-mood listener

**Less ideal for**:
- Music: detail-heavy electronic, metal, technical genres
- Use: critical mixing / mastering reference
- Personality: detail-obsessed audiophile

**Upgrade path**: Pure → [Aria 2]({{< ref "reviews/moondrop-aria-2-review" >}}) ($89, same tier different brand) → [Tanchjim Origin]({{< ref "reviews/tanchjim-origin-review" >}}) ($180)

## Combo C — Phone-First Setup ($89)

### [Moondrop Aria 2]({{< ref "reviews/moondrop-aria-2-review" >}}) ($89) — Phone-driven

**Why this combo**: If you don't want to think about source upgrades yet, the Aria 2 is the **single best single-DD IEM that sounds great from a phone**. No dongle needed (though one is welcome later).

**Sound character**: Warm-leaning, smooth, single-DD timbre. Coherent across all frequencies. Very forgiving.

**Best for**:
- Music: everything except technical death metal
- Use: commute, casual listening, "just plays music"
- Personality: pragmatic, prefers ergonomics over absolute fidelity

**Less ideal for**:

- Personality: future audiophile collector (this is a "do-it-all", not a "do-it-best")

**Upgrade path**: Aria 2 → Aria 2 + Fosi DS2 → eventually [Nova]({{< ref "reviews/truthear-nova-review" >}}) ($150)

## What about cables?

**Don't worry about cables for the first $200**. The included cables on all 4 IEMs are workable. Cable upgrades are a $100+ tier concern after you've settled into the hobby. If you upgrade later, the standard 0.78mm 2-pin and 4.4mm balanced cables are what to look for.

## What about tips?

**Get 1-2 tip alternatives** ($10-20 total):
- **JVC Spiral Dots** ($12 for 3 pairs) — different soundstage character
- **Final Type E** ($15 for 3 pairs) — comfort + better mids

Tip rolling changes sound more than cable changes for most listeners. Budget $20 for this experimentation.

## What about EQ?

**Don't EQ on day 1**. Let yourself acclimatize to the IEM's natural tuning for 2-3 weeks before EQ-ing. After that:

- **iOS users**: Apple Music EQ (limited), Equilibrator (third-party)
- **Android users**: Wavelet, Spotify EQ, ViPER4Android
- **Desktop users**: EqualizerAPO (Windows), eqMac (macOS)

The most-used EQ profile in r/iems is **dropping the 7-9 kHz peak by 2-3 dB** for analytical IEMs (Hexa, S12 Pro). Try it after acclimatization.

## Common newbie mistakes

**Mistake 1: Buying $50 cheap IEMs first to "test the waters"**.
Reality: $50 IEMs (KZ DQ6, TRN MT1) are objectively worse than $80 IEMs (Hexa, Aria 2). You'll buy the $80 IEMs eventually anyway. Just start there.

**Mistake 2: Buying without a source plan**.
A $160 planar IEM with no DAC is wasted budget. Either skip planars until you have a DAC, or start with a hybrid (Hexa, Aria 2) that works from phones.

**Mistake 3: Reading too many reviews**.
Analysis paralysis kills the hobby. **Pick a combo from this guide. Buy it. Listen for 6-8 weeks.** Then you'll know what you actually want next.

**Mistake 4: Buying multi-driver "flagships" first**.
A 5-driver $120 IEM is rarely better than a 1+3 driver $80 IEM. Driver count is marketing.

**Mistake 5: Skipping the dongle DAC**.
The $40 Fosi DS2 delivers more audible improvement than upgrading the IEM by $40. Source matters.

## Where this leaves you

You've picked a combo. You've ordered. Now:

1. **First week**: Listen to your favorite music. Notice what's different from your phone's stock buds.
2. **Week 2-3**: Try the alternative tips. Note which work best for your ear shape.
3. **Week 4-6**: Identify if you want more detail (upgrade to Combo A if you started with C) or more warmth (downgrade to Combo B if you started with A).
4. **Month 2+**: Read about EQ. Try the 7-9 kHz drop on analytical IEMs.
5. **Month 3+**: Consider upgrade path — [next-step IEMs at $200]({{< ref "guides/best-chifi-iems-under-200-2026" >}}) or [source upgrades]({{< ref "guides/best-chifi-daps-2026" >}}).
