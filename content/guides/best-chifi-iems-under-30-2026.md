The under-$30 tier is **the most important entry point for new audiophiles**. Get a bad first IEM and you walk away thinking ChiFi is hype. Get a good one and you're hooked.

This guide covers **the IEMs we'd actually buy under $30 in 2026**, with honest takes on what they do well and where they compromise.

## TL;DR — pick by use case

- **Best overall**: [Moondrop Chu 2]({{< ref "reviews/moondrop-chu-2-review" >}}) ($20) — single-DD reference
- **Best with detachable cable**: [Truthear Gate]({{< ref "reviews/truthear-gate-review" >}}) ($22) — modular = upgradeable
- **Best build quality**: [KZ Castor]({{< ref "reviews/kz-castor-review" >}}) ($20-30) — tuning switches included
- **Best for first BA experience**: [Tangzu Waner SG]({{< ref "reviews/tangzu-waner-sg-review" >}}) ($20) — premium build
- **Best for treble-sensitive**: [7Hz Salnotes Zero]({{< ref "reviews/7hz-salnotes-zero-review" >}}) ($20) — smooth Harman target
- **Best value at $25-30**: NF Audio NA3 ($30) — bigger driver, more refined

## What to expect at this tier

For $20-30, you get:
- **Surprisingly competent tuning** — entry-tier ChiFi often matches $80-100 mainstream brands
- **Plastic / resin shells** — no premium materials yet
- **Basic cables** — workable, often non-detachable
- **Minimal accessories** — silicone tips, basic case if you're lucky

You don't get:
- Multi-driver hybrids (need to go up to $50+ for those)
- Premium accessories (foam tips, leather cases, modular cables)
- Best-in-class detail retrieval

The key thing at this tier: **find the tuning you like**. Detail is a marginal concern; ergonomics + tonality matter more.

## The list

### #1 — Moondrop Chu 2 ($20) — Best overall

**Single 10mm dynamic driver, refined Moondrop tuning, non-detachable cable**

[Read our Chu 2 review]({{< ref "reviews/moondrop-chu-2-review" >}}).

The Chu 2 is **the default sub-$30 first IEM**. Moondrop's VDSF tuning at a third the price of the Aria 2 — slightly less detail but the same tonal philosophy.

**Why it wins**:
- Single-DD coherence (no crossover artifacts)
- Forgiving tuning — works for almost all genres
- Premium brand DNA at entry price
- Comfortable resin shell

**Weakness**: cable is fixed (not detachable). If the cable breaks, the IEM is dead.

**Best for**: first audiophile IEM purchase, comfortable all-around

---

### #2 — Truthear Gate ($22) — Best with detachable cable

**Single 10mm DD, detachable 0.78mm 2-pin cable**

[Read our Gate review]({{< ref "reviews/truthear-gate-review" >}}) · [Chu 2 vs Gate]({{< ref "vs/moondrop-chu-2-vs-truthear-gate" >}}).

The Gate is **the same value as Chu 2 + detachable cable**. Tuning is very similar; the detachable cable is the headline feature.

**Why it wins**:
- Future-proof (replace cable when it breaks)
- Sound quality matches Chu 2
- Truthear / Crinacle tuning collaboration
- Allows balanced (4.4mm) cable upgrade path

**Weakness**: shell less premium-feeling than Chu 2.

**Best for**: long-term ownership, those wanting cable flexibility

### #3 — KZ Castor ($20-30) — Best build with tuning switches

**Single DD + tuning switches (bass/treble), detachable cable**

[Read our Castor review]({{< ref "reviews/kz-castor-review" >}}).

KZ has historically been hit-or-miss, but the Castor is **their best-tuned recent release**. The shell is premium metal, build is sturdy, and the tuning switches let you swap between three different signatures.

**Why it wins**:
- Highest build quality at this tier
- Tuning flexibility (3 switch positions)
- Best for "I want to try different sounds" curiosity buyers

**Weakness**: KZ brand reputation (older models had QC issues). This one is good.

**Best for**: those wanting build durability + tuning experimentation

### #4 — Tangzu Waner SG ($20) — Best premium build

**Single DD, premium shell, classic Harman tuning**

[Read our Waner SG review]({{< ref "reviews/tangzu-waner-sg-review" >}}).

Tangzu's Waner SG punches well above its price on build quality. The shell has an "S.G." (Silver Gradient) finish that looks like a $50+ IEM.

**Why it wins**:
- Premium-feeling build at entry price
- Solid Harman-target tuning
- Comfortable shell shape

**Weakness**: tuning is slightly bass-shy; treble-sensitive might find it a touch bright.

**Best for**: those who want "looks expensive" first IEM

### #5 — 7Hz Salnotes Zero ($20) — Best for treble-sensitive

**Single DD, Harman target, smooth treble**

[Read our Salnotes Zero review]({{< ref "reviews/7hz-salnotes-zero-review" >}}).

The Salnotes Zero hit the market in 2023 and **set a new sub-$30 standard**. Single-DD with intentionally smooth treble — perfect for listeners who find other IEMs harsh.

**Why it wins**:
- Smoothest sub-$30 treble
- Excellent for vocal-heavy and acoustic listening
- Long sessions without fatigue

**Weakness**: less sparkle / detail than Chu 2 or Gate. Good for "I want to relax" listening; less good for "I want to hear everything" analytical listening.

**Best for**: treble-sensitive, vocal-led music, long sessions

### #6 — NF Audio NA3 ($30) — Best at the top of tier

**Bigger 11mm DD, premium tuning, detachable cable**

[Read our NA3 review]({{< ref "reviews/nf-audio-na3-review" >}}).

At $30, the NA3 sits at the upper edge of this tier. The 11mm DD delivers more bass extension and macro-dynamics than 10mm IEMs at lower prices.

## What to skip at this tier

- **5-driver+ "marketing IEMs"** (KZ ZS10 Pro, etc.) — driver count doesn't help, tuning matters
- **TRN MT1 Pro** — Reddit consensus is "skip"
- **Bluetooth-only IEMs** in this tier — latency + lossy codec ruins the value
- **"Gaming" branded IEMs** (HyperX, Razer) — overpriced for sound quality

## Buying tips

**Tip 1: Source matters even at this tier.** Pair with **at least the [Apple USB-C dongle]({{< ref "guides/best-dongle-dacs-2026" >}})** ($9) for proper performance. Phone headphone-jack outputs vary wildly.

**Tip 2: Tip rolling helps.** Stock tips on $20 IEMs are often mediocre. Budget $5-10 for a JVC Spiral Dots or Final Type E set — sound improves noticeably.

**Tip 3: Don't expect detail.** At $20-30, you get coherent tuning, not maximum resolution. Want detail? Save up for $79 Hexa.

**Tip 4: Burn-in is mostly placebo.** Some claim 50+ hours of "burn-in" — measurements don't support this. Listen and decide quickly; if it doesn't work in week 1, return / sell.

**Tip 5: Cables don't matter at this price.** Cable upgrades only become audible at $100+ IEMs. Stock cable is fine for any $20-30 IEM.

## Upgrade path

Once you've spent 2-3 months with an entry-tier IEM:

1. **Add a [dongle DAC]({{< ref "guides/best-dongle-dacs-2026" >}})** ($40 Fosi DS2) — more audible improvement than buying a new IEM
2. **Upgrade to [Hexa or Aria 2]({{< ref "guides/best-chifi-iems-under-100-2026" >}})** ($79-89) — meaningful sonic step up
3. **Consider [Truthear Nova or S12 Pro]({{< ref "guides/best-chifi-iems-under-200-2026" >}})** ($150-160) — mid-tier reference

The smart progression skips intermediate tiers. From $20 → $80 → $160 is the canonical path. Don't waste money on $40-60 IEMs that are barely better than $20 ones.
