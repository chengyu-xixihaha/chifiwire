ThieAudio quietly dropped the **Hype 4 MKII** in late May 2026 at **$399** — the same price as the original — but with a significantly different tuning philosophy. This is not a minor revision. The MKII is a deliberate move toward a brighter, more U-shaped, more technically resolving sound that departs from the warmer, more relaxed character the original Hype 4 built its fanbase on.

Fourteen expert reviewers have weighed in so far, averaging **7.6 out of 10**.

## What Changed vs the Original Hype 4

The driver architecture is the same on paper — two dynamic drivers plus four balanced armatures per side — but the crossover and tuning are substantially different:

- **Sub-bass**: a pronounced ~9dB shelf centered around 50Hz adds more rumble and physical impact than the original
- **Treble**: extended further with a brighter character; the MKII has noticeably more air and sparkle
- **Overall shape**: moves from the original's more neutral-warm signature to a clearer U-shape (elevated bass + elevated treble, recessed relative midrange)
- **Technical performance**: reviewers consistently note improved detail retrieval and staging relative to the first version

The CNC aluminum housing is unchanged in form, but the tuning inside it is a different product.

## What Reviewers Are Saying

Across 14 reviews, the MKII scores **7.6/10** on average — a competitive result for the $300–500 hybrid tier.

The consistent positives: sub-bass texture and quantity, treble extension and detail, build quality, and the modular cable inclusion at this price point. The consistent caveat: the MKII's tuning is polarizing for listeners who loved the original. If your preference runs warm-neutral with smooth highs, the MKII is a meaningful departure, not an upgrade.

Reviewers who prefer technical IEMs with a V/U-shaped bias tend to rank it higher. Those coming from the original — or from house sounds like Harman-neutral — tend to rate it closer to the bottom of that 7.6 average.

## Should You Upgrade from the Original Hype 4?

The short answer: **only if you want a brighter, more energetic sound**.

The MKII is strictly better in raw technical terms — more detail, better treble extension, more impactful sub-bass. But "better technically" does not mean "better for you." The original Hype 4's warmer, more forgiving tuning has a real audience, and the MKII does not serve that audience.

If you listen to genres that benefit from elevated sub-bass and air — electronic, hip-hop, metal — the MKII's U-shape is purpose-built for you. If you prefer vocals, acoustic, or jazz, and the original's tuning worked well in that context, the MKII is a lateral move at best.

New buyers at $399 are getting a competitive hybrid IEM with premium hardware (Knowles drivers, CNC aluminum, modular cable) and a tuning that sits confidently in the enthusiast mainstream. The 7.6/10 consensus reflects a solid IEM that is not without tradeoffs — which is accurate for this price tier.

The Hype 4 MKII is available now through Linsoul, HiFiGo, and ThieAudio's direct store.
