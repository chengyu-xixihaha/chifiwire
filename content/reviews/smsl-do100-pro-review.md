The **SMSL DO100 Pro** at $219 packs more inputs and features into a $200 DAC than anything else in the segment. Dual ES9039Q2M chips, **HDMI ARC** (yes, plug your TV into it), USB, optical, coaxial, Bluetooth 5.1 with LDAC, hardware MQA decoding, balanced XLR output. The kind of spec sheet that should cost $400, not $220.

Short version: **if you have multiple sources (PC, console, TV, phone) and want one DAC handling all of them, the DO100 Pro is the clear pick under $300.** If you only have one source, save $20 and get the Topping E50 II instead.

## Specs at a glance

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| **DAC chip** | **Dual ES9039Q2M** (one per channel — flagship-tier ESS) |
| **USB controller** | XMOS XU316 |
| **Inputs** | USB, optical (Toslink), coaxial, Bluetooth 5.1, **HDMI ARC** |
| **Bluetooth chip** | Qualcomm QCC5125, LDAC 24-bit/96kHz, aptX-HD, aptX, AAC, SBC |
| **Outputs** | RCA single-ended, **balanced XLR**, line-out only (no headphone jack) |
| **PCM support** | Up to 768 kHz / 32-bit |
| **DSD support** | Native DSD512, DoP256 |
| **MQA** | Hardware 8x unfolding (USB, optical, coaxial) |
| **THD+N** | 0.00008% (ASR measured) |
| **MSRP** | $219 |

The standout: **HDMI ARC** is rare at this price. Plug your TV's HDMI ARC port into the DO100 Pro and your TV's audio (including Apple TV, PS5, Xbox content) plays through your hi-fi setup.

## Who it's for

- **Home theater integrator** who wants TV audio in their hi-fi setup
- **Multi-source desk**: PC + phone + console + TV all into one DAC
- **MQA streamer** who still believes in MQA (uncommon in 2026, but valid)
- **ESS lover** who prefers Sabre's neutral house sound over AKM's Velvet

Skip it if:
- You only have one source (PC OR phone, not both — Topping E50 II saves $20)
- You want a headphone amp built-in (DO100 Pro has no headphone jack)
- You hate ESS "house sound" (some find Sabre chips too clinical — Topping E50 II's AKM is warmer)

## Build, aesthetics, ergonomics

The DO100 Pro is a **half-rack-width brick** — about the footprint of a hardback book laid flat, ~2" tall. Aluminum chassis, brushed finish, large color OLED display on front.

Display shows: input, sample rate, bit depth, MQA status, volume. Rotary knob handles volume + input selection. Remote control included (rare at this price).

The build is **a step up from Topping's E50 II** in feel — the chassis is thicker, the buttons more substantial, the display larger and more informative. You can tell SMSL invested in industrial design.

Color options: black or silver, both with white display text.

## The HDMI ARC story

Most desktop DACs assume your sources are USB (PC) or Bluetooth (phone). The DO100 Pro is one of the few **under $500** with HDMI ARC.

What HDMI ARC gets you:
- **TV → DAC**: Your TV audio (Netflix, Apple TV+, Plex on TV) plays through your hi-fi
- **PS5 / Xbox / Switch via TV**: console audio routed through DAC
- **No optical bottleneck**: HDMI ARC supports higher quality than optical (Toslink caps at 192 kHz; HDMI carries lossless)
- **CEC remote pass-through**: your TV remote can control DAC volume (limited but works)

If you have a serious 2-channel hi-fi setup and use your TV often, this single feature justifies the DO100 Pro over the E50 II.

## Sound

**Transparent — same conclusion as every competent DAC at this tier.** ASR measurements show the DO100 Pro hitting THD+N of 0.00008% (literally beyond audible thresholds).

What you'll notice:
- **Dead neutral** — the DAC doesn't impose a tonality on your music
- **Dead silent** noise floor at any reasonable volume
- **Excellent dynamic range** with both quiet and loud passages clean
- **Imaging** preserved with high precision (dual mono DAC topology helps)

What you won't notice (vs. a $1,000 DAC):
- Any audible difference. Period.

The "dual mono" topology — one ES9039Q2M per channel — is genuinely better for measurements but the audible benefit at this price is hard to argue.

## Bluetooth quality

LDAC at 24-bit/96kHz over Bluetooth 5.1 is **the best phone-to-DAC wireless option in 2026**. Pair an Android phone (LDAC), select "best quality" in Developer Options, and stream Tidal Master to your hi-fi rig without wires.

Pairing is fast, drops are rare in normal use. The Qualcomm QCC5125 is the same chip used in many premium Bluetooth audio products.

## Compared to

### vs. Topping E50 II ($200)

E50 II is $20 cheaper, has AKM AK4497S (warmer character vs ESS), and offers 10-band hardware PEQ via app. DO100 Pro has **HDMI ARC, more inputs, dual mono ESS topology, and a better remote**. **Toss-up by use case** — multi-source = DO100 Pro, PEQ enthusiast = E50 II.

### vs. SMSL DO200 MKII ($499)

DO200 MKII is the bigger sibling — dual ES9068AS, dual XMOS, more powerful balanced output. **Worth the 2x only if you have studio monitors with low-level inputs.** For headphone listening, DO100 Pro is identical-sounding.

### vs. SMSL D-6 ($179)

D-6 is the budget version — single ES9038Q2M, fewer inputs, no HDMI ARC, no MQA. **Save $40, lose features.** Get the DO100 Pro unless your needs are minimal.

### vs. FiiO K7 ($219)

K7 is integrated DAC + headphone amp. **If you only care about headphones at desktop, K7 is the smarter pick** (no need for separate amp). DO100 Pro is for those running active speakers OR planning a separate dedicated headphone amp.

## Verdict

**Pros**
- ✅ Dual ES9039Q2M flagship-tier DAC chips (one per channel)
- ✅ **HDMI ARC** — rare at this price, real value for TV audio
- ✅ 5 inputs covered (USB, optical, coax, Bluetooth, HDMI)
- ✅ Bluetooth 5.1 with LDAC at 24-bit/96kHz
- ✅ Hardware MQA decoding (8x unfold)
- ✅ Balanced XLR output for active speakers / separate amp
- ✅ Included remote control
- ✅ Excellent display and build

**Cons**
- ❌ No headphone jack (line-out only)
- ❌ ESS Sabre "house sound" is too clinical for some (AKM-preferring listeners → E50 II)
- ❌ At $220, $20 more than E50 II — only worth it if you need HDMI ARC
- ❌ MQA hardware decoding is fading relevance (MQA company restructured in 2025)

**Buy if:** you have a multi-source desk including a TV, you stream from phone via Bluetooth, you want flagship-tier ESS measurements at $220.

**Skip if:** you only have one source (USB-only PC users → E50 II), you need a headphone amp built in (K7), or you specifically prefer AKM tonality.
