The Tangzu Wan'er S.G. occupies an odd position in the $20 IEM market — **arguably as good as the Moondrop Chu 2 and Truthear Gate, but consistently overlooked** because Tangzu is the less recognized name. The "S.G." stands for "Sweetie Girl" (Tangzu's branding is... distinctive), which doesn't help.

If you can get past the name, the Wan'er S.G. delivers a **warmer, more bass-forward** tuning than the Chu 2 / Gate at the same price — making it the right pick for specific listener profiles.

Short version: **the Wan'er is the warmth-leaning option in the $20 tier**. Read on for whether that fits you.

## Specs at a glance

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Driver** | Single 10mm dynamic, custom PET diaphragm |
| **Impedance** | 32 Ω |
| **Sensitivity** | 110 dB @ 1kHz |
| **Frequency response** | 20 Hz – 20 kHz |
| **Cable** | Detachable 0.78mm 2-pin, 3.5mm SE termination |
| **Shell** | Resin (semi-translucent finish in some colors) |
| **Accessories** | 3 sets silicone tips, soft pouch |
| **Tuning** | Warm-leaning Harman variant |
| **MSRP** | $19.99 |

The Wan'er S.G. matches the Truthear Gate's value proposition — detachable cable, single DD, sub-$25 — and adds a **slightly warmer tuning** that gives it a distinct profile.

## Who it's for

- **First IEM buyer** who prefers warmth over neutrality
- **Rock / jazz / acoustic listener** — Wan'er's warmth flatters these genres
- **Older listener** with treble sensitivity (Wan'er treble is polite)
- **Long-session listener** — the warmth profile is fatigue-free
- **Brand-name agnostic buyer** open to less famous brands

Skip it if:
- You want maximum technical performance at $20 (Truthear Gate edges on detail)
- You want metal shell (Chu 2 wins on build)
- You want maximum sub-bass slam (Truthear Zero Red at $55 is the bass pick)

## Build, fit, accessories

The Wan'er's **resin shell is lightweight** — comparable to the Truthear Gate's, slightly more than the Salnotes Zero. Available in semi-translucent colors (Sky Blue, Princess Pink, others) — divisive aesthetically but unique among the same-priced competitors that all default to black.

Ergonomics are very good — universal shape, smooth surfaces, no pressure points on long wear.

The **detachable 2-pin cable** is the practical highlight. Standard 0.78mm spacing. Any aftermarket cable works. Stock cable is functional but plain.

Accessories: 3 silicone tip sets + a soft pouch. Same as the Gate and Salnotes Zero — basic but expected at $20.

## Sound

The Wan'er's tuning is **warmer than the Chu 2, Gate, or Salnotes Zero**. More mid-bass body, more lower-mid warmth, slightly polite treble. The result is "musical" rather than "reference" — coloring music with a slight golden hue.

### Bass

**More mid-bass than any other $20 IEM**. Kick drums have weight; bass guitar lines have body and warmth. Sub-bass extension is comparable to the Chu 2 — present but not slammy.

For rock, jazz, and acoustic music, this tuning **flatters the genre**. For neutral / analytical listening, it adds coloration.

### Mids

**Lower-mid emphasis** gives male vocals and rock guitars added body. Female vocals are appropriately forward without becoming shouty.

The slight warmth in the mids makes **older recordings** (60s/70s rock, jazz standards) sound more "right" — warmer than the cooler digital tuning of newer competitors.

### Treble

**Polite, smooth, never piercing**. Similar character to the Salnotes Zero. Hi-hats are present without sparkle; cymbals decay without crispness.

For treble-sensitive listeners, this is a feature. For sparkle-seekers, look elsewhere (EW200 or Gate).

### Soundstage and imaging

**Intimate stage, decent imaging**. Comparable to the Chu 2 — close vocal placement, instruments stay relatively centered. The warmth profile doesn't help width perception.

### Technical performance

Class-appropriate. Detail retrieval is fine without being noteworthy. Transient response is slightly slower than the Truthear Gate's LCP diaphragm — not a fatal flaw at $20, but the EW200 ($40) is clearly better on speed.

## Source — phone is fine

110 dB sensitivity + 32 Ω = **drives easily from any source**. Phone, dongle, anything works. Doesn't scale meaningfully with better sources.

## Why it's overlooked (and shouldn't be)

The Wan'er S.G. **doesn't have the marketing of Moondrop or Truthear**. Tangzu's brand presence in English-speaking audiophile communities is smaller. Most "best $20 IEM" lists default to the Chu 2 or Gate because of name recognition.

The actual sound difference between the three:

- **Chu 2**: vocal-forward, neutral, fixed cable
- **Gate**: slightly U-shaped, treble-forward, detachable cable
- **Wan'er**: warm-leaning, mid-bass-emphasized, detachable cable

**Three different flavors, same price**. Pick by signature preference, not by brand recognition.

## Compared to

### vs. Moondrop Chu 2 ($20)

Chu 2 has metal shell (vs resin) and fixed cable (vs detachable). Tuning is more neutral / vocal-forward. **Pick Chu 2 for build feel; pick Wan'er for warmth and cable swap-ability**.

[Read our Chu 2 review](/reviews/moondrop-chu-2-review/).

### vs. Truthear Gate ($22)

Both detachable, both resin. **Wan'er is warmer; Gate is more energetic** (more treble, more sub-bass). Pick by preference.

[Read our Gate review](/reviews/truthear-gate-review/).

### vs. 7Hz Salnotes Zero ($25)

The closest competitor in tuning style — both warm-leaning. **Wan'er is $5 cheaper; Salnotes Zero has slightly more refined build** (metal nozzle). Slight edge to Salnotes for community validation; Wan'er for value.

[Read our Salnotes Zero review](/reviews/7hz-salnotes-zero-review/).

### vs. KZ Castor Harman ($35)

Castor is 75% more expensive but **has tuning switches and better mid-bass control**. **Wan'er is the value pick under $25; Castor is the upgrade if you want adjustability**.

## Verdict

**Pros**
- ✅ Warmest tuning in the $20 tier — flatters rock / jazz / acoustic
- ✅ Detachable 2-pin cable, future-proof
- ✅ Lightweight resin, comfortable for hours
- ✅ Unique color options (semi-translucent)
- ✅ Smooth treble — non-fatiguing

**Cons**
- ❌ Less brand recognition (no community validation safety net)
- ❌ Resin shell less premium than Chu 2's metal
- ❌ Mids less forward than Chu 2 for vocal music
- ❌ Treble lacks sparkle (similar to Salnotes Zero)
- ❌ Only 3 sets of tips included

**Buy if:** you want a warmer signature at $20, you listen to rock / jazz / acoustic / older recordings, and you don't need brand-name reassurance.

**Skip if:** you want metal build (Chu 2), neutral tuning (Chu 2), or maximum treble detail (Gate or EW200).
