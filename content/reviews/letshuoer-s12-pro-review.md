The original Letshuoer S12 ($150) was the IEM that put planar-magnetic drivers on the audiophile budget map back in 2022. The **S12 Pro** is its refined sibling — same 14.8mm planar driver concept, better tuning, better accessories, and a modular cable in the box. At $160, it competes with hybrid and dynamic IEMs that are sonically very different. The question for buyers: **is planar the right pick for you, and is the S12 Pro the right planar?**

Short answer: **if you want maximum detail and speed in a sub-$200 IEM, and you don't mind a V-shaped signature, the S12 Pro is the technical benchmark.** Read on for caveats.

## Specs at a glance

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Driver** | Single 14.8mm planar magnetic |
| **Impedance** | 16 Ω |
| **Sensitivity** | 102 dB/mW (relatively low — see "Source" below) |
| **Frequency response** | 20 Hz – 40 kHz |
| **Shell** | CNC-machined aluminum, 5-axis finish |
| **Cable** | 0.78mm 2-pin detachable, silver-plated monocrystalline copper, 4×98-strand |
| **Cable termination** | **Modular — 2.5mm + 3.5mm + 4.4mm plugs all included** |
| **Tuning** | V-shaped, sub-bass forward, treble extended |
| **Accessories** | Memory foam + standard + transparent tips (3 sizes each), faux leather case |
| **MSRP** | $159.99 |

The standout: **planar driver at this price**, with a **fully modular cable** (three plugs, not just two like the Aria 2). The fit-and-finish is well above the price tag.

## Who it's for

- **Anyone who wants the cleanest, fastest transient response under $200** — that's the planar advantage
- **Listeners moving up from single-DD IEMs** who want technical capability they can hear immediately
- **People with a real source** (DAP or dongle DAC) — phone-only listeners should skip
- **V-shape fans** — if you like elevated bass + elevated treble, this delivers

Skip it if:
- You only listen on phones (under-driven planars sound flat and lifeless)
- You hate treble peaks — the S12 Pro has presence-region energy that some find too much
- You want lush mids — V-shaped tuning leaves the mids recessed by design

## Build, fit, accessories

The aluminum shell is **excellent at the price** — sandblasted matte finish, no sharp edges, light enough for hours of wear. The 5-axis CNC machining means the ergonomics actually match the concha of the ear, unlike many cheap IEMs that just bolt a generic shape onto a driver.

The **modular cable is the headline accessory**: SPC monocrystalline copper, four braided 98-strand cores, soft and low microphonic. Three plugs (2.5mm balanced, 3.5mm single-ended, 4.4mm balanced) screw on with a metal collar — confident, not flimsy. You can A/B SE vs balanced in 5 seconds.

Three sets of tips (memory foam, standard silicone, transparent) plus carrying case round out a complete package. Most $160 IEMs ship with one set of generic tips and a cable upgrade nag — this skips that.

## Sound

The S12 Pro is **V-shaped with a planar twist**. Bass and treble are elevated; mids sit slightly back; everything is fast and clean because of the driver type.

### Bass

Sub-bass is the standout. The 14.8mm planar diaphragm has the surface area to move air, and Letshuoer tuned for **deep, textured, rumbling sub-bass** without bleed into the mids. Mid-bass is tighter — kick drums hit but don't linger.

**Listen to "Strobe" by Deadmau5 or "Limit to Your Love" by James Blake** to hear what this driver can do. You will not believe this came from a $160 IEM.

The trade-off: bass guitars and acoustic basses can sound slightly *too* clean — some warmth and natural decay is sacrificed for speed.

### Mids

Recessed. Female vocals are clear but lack the air a Moondrop Aria 2 gives them. Male vocals have appropriate weight. Guitars in a rock mix can sound slightly distant compared to a more mid-forward IEM.

This is the V-shape compromise. If you live in the mids — folk, jazz vocals, classical chamber — the S12 Pro is not your IEM.

### Treble

Bright, extended, detailed. The planar driver excels here — hi-hats glisten, cymbals decay naturally, micro-detail in the upper register is exceptional for the price.

The trade-off: a presence-region peak around 7-8 kHz that some listeners find sharp on harsh recordings. **Pre-amp -2 dB at 7 kHz tames it for those affected**. Most users adapt within a week.

### Soundstage and imaging

Wide stage, good depth, excellent layering. The planar's fast transients give you clear instrument positions; close your eyes and you can point at the drummer. **Better than most sub-$200 IEMs**, including the Truthear Hexa.

### Technical performance

Speed, detail, separation — all at a level you usually don't see until $300+. The planar advantage is real and audible.

## Source — this matters

**102 dB/mW sensitivity is on the lower end** for a modern IEM. A phone headphone jack or a basic dongle (like a $9 Apple USB-C dongle) will drive the S12 Pro to volume but **the bass won't develop** — it'll sound thin and flat.

Plug it into a Moondrop Dawn Pro, a FiiO JM21, or any balanced output at ~150 mW+ and the S12 Pro transforms. **Use the included 4.4mm balanced cable if your source has it**.

This is the single biggest reason people return the S12 Pro: they tried it on a phone, weren't impressed, and refunded. If you don't have a real source, save your money or buy an Aria 2 instead.

## Compared to

### vs. Truthear Hexa ($79)

The Hexa is half the price, hybrid tribrid, more "musical reference". The S12 Pro is technically superior — more detail, faster transients, deeper sub-bass, wider stage. But Hexa is more forgiving on bad sources and bad recordings. **Get the Hexa first if you're new; upgrade to S12 Pro when you have proper gear.**

### vs. Moondrop Aria 2 ($89)

Different leagues technically. Aria 2 is warmer, more midrange-forward, easier listening. S12 Pro is sharper, faster, more detailed but V-shaped. **Pick by signature**: warm and lush = Aria 2, neutral-bright and analytical = S12 Pro.

### vs. Tin HiFi P2 Plus ($85)

The P2 Plus is the budget planar challenger. It's competent but the S12 Pro has better build, better cable, better tuning, and more sub-bass slam. **The price premium is justified.**

### vs. Letshuoer S12 Ultra ($300+)

The Ultra is the next step up — same driver, more refined tuning, premium accessories. Not worth 2x the price unless you've already maxed out the S12 Pro and want the last 10%.

## Verdict

**Pros**
- ✅ Class-leading planar driver at the price
- ✅ Deep, textured, fast sub-bass
- ✅ Wide soundstage, excellent imaging
- ✅ Fully modular cable (2.5/3.5/4.4mm)
- ✅ Premium aluminum build, comfortable fit
- ✅ Three full sets of tips included

**Cons**
- ❌ Needs a real source (not phone-friendly)
- ❌ V-shape leaves mids recessed
- ❌ 7-8 kHz peak can be sharp for some
- ❌ Stock cable, while modular, isn't as premium-feeling as the IEMs deserve

**Buy if:** you have a DAP or dongle DAC, you want maximum technical performance under $200, and you like a V-shape with deep sub-bass and clean treble.

**Skip if:** you only have a phone, you prioritize warm mids and vocal-forward tuning, or you're treble-sensitive and won't EQ.
