The 7Hz Salnotes Zero launched in 2023 and immediately became **the default $25 IEM recommendation across r/iems, Head-Fi, and most audiophile YouTube channels**. Three years later it's still on best-buys lists — which says everything about how solid the original execution was.

Short version: **the Salnotes Zero is the $25 IEM you can buy without research**. It does everything right for the price and nothing offensively wrong. Read on for who'd prefer it over the newer competitors.

## Specs at a glance

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Driver** | Single 10mm dynamic, polymer composite diaphragm |
| **Impedance** | 32 Ω |
| **Sensitivity** | 108 dB @ 1kHz |
| **Frequency response** | 20 Hz – 20 kHz |
| **Cable** | Detachable 0.78mm 2-pin, 3.5mm SE termination |
| **Shell** | Resin with metal nozzle |
| **Accessories** | 3 sets silicone tips, basic carrying pouch |
| **Tuning** | Harman-derived, slightly warmer than Chu 2 / Gate |
| **MSRP** | $24.99 |

The Salnotes Zero was **the first sub-$30 IEM with detachable cable + Harman-derived tuning** to genuinely hit broad community approval. Newer competitors (Truthear Gate, Tangzu Wan'er) have matched or exceeded on specific metrics, but the Zero remains a balanced all-rounder.

## Who it's for

- **First audiophile IEM** for someone who doesn't want to research at all
- **Long-session listeners** who hate fatiguing treble
- **Warmth-leaning listeners** who find the Chu 2 / Gate slightly thin
- **Detachable cable enthusiasts** at the lowest possible price
- **Gift buyer** to a friend who wants something "better than AirPods"

Skip it if:
- You want the absolute sharpest treble or detail (Simgot EW200 at $40 is better)
- You're a bass-head (Truthear Zero Red at $55 has way more slam)
- You want maximum technical performance (any tribrid above $80)

## Build, fit, accessories

The Salnotes Zero's **resin shell is featherweight** — even lighter than the Truthear Gate. The shape is universal — works for small and large ears equally. Long sessions (3+ hours) feel almost transparent.

The detachable 2-pin cable uses **standard 0.78mm spacing**, so any aftermarket cable fits. Stock cable is **slightly thinner than the Gate's** but still acceptable.

Accessories are basic: 3 silicone tip sets + a fabric pouch. Same as the Gate. Budget another $8-15 for better tips.

The metal nozzle is a build detail you'll appreciate — many sub-$30 IEMs use plastic nozzles that wear quickly. The Salnotes Zero's nozzle survives long-term use.

## Sound

The Salnotes Zero is **slightly warmer than the Chu 2 or Gate** — more sub-bass body, more midrange warmth, slightly less treble emphasis. The result is a "relaxed audiophile" tuning that works on most music without ever becoming intense.

### Bass

**More sub-bass body than the Chu 2**. Not slammy like the Truthear Zero Red, but you can hear and feel the bottom octave. Mid-bass is similar — tight, present without bleeding.

For rock, jazz, and acoustic-with-bass-guitar, this tuning is well-judged. For EDM, the Truthear Gate has more punch.

### Mids

**The warmest mids in the sub-$30 tier**. Male vocals especially benefit — they have body and chest resonance that the Chu 2's leaner tuning lacks. Female vocals are appropriately forward but less "airy" than the Chu 2's VDSF tuning.

If you listen to a lot of jazz vocals or older rock recordings, the Salnotes Zero's warmth makes them feel right.

### Treble

**Smooth, polite, never piercing**. Cymbals and hi-hats are present but rolled-off above ~10 kHz. Some listeners call this "dark" — for sub-$30, "smooth" is more accurate.

Trade-off: **lacks excitement on cymbal-heavy genres**. If you live in jazz or want sparkle on classical strings, the Truthear Gate or Simgot EW200 gives you more.

### Soundstage and imaging

**Intimate stage, decent imaging**. Comparable to the Chu 2 — close vocal placement, instruments stay relatively centered. Not the widest sub-$30 IEM (the Gate edges it slightly) but accurate within its width.

### Technical performance

Class-appropriate. The Salnotes Zero won't reveal hidden layers in your library — but it also won't smear them. Transient response is reasonable for a single DD. Detail retrieval is fine without being notable.

## Source — phone is fine

108 dB sensitivity is **slightly less efficient** than the Chu 2 (120 dB) or Gate (120 dB). On phone you'll set the volume slightly higher. Still drives easily from any source — no real power requirement.

Doesn't scale meaningfully with better sources. The Salnotes Zero is what it is from any output.

## Why it's still relevant in 2026

Three years after launch, the Zero is still recommended because:

1. **Tuning is genre-flexible**: works for almost everything, offends nothing
2. **Build is reliable**: low warranty rate, predictable QC
3. **Cable is detachable + standard**: future-proof in a category where most products are disposable
4. **Price held**: still $25, hasn't crept up like some long-tail products

Newer competitors (Truthear Gate, Tangzu Wan'er) have arguably surpassed the Zero on specific metrics, but **none have all-around bested it**. It remains the safe default.

## Compared to

### vs. Moondrop Chu 2 ($20)

Chu 2 is $5 cheaper, has metal shell, fixed cable. Zero is detachable, slightly warmer. **Pick Zero for cable longevity; pick Chu 2 for vocal forwardness and metal feel**.

### vs. Truthear Gate ($22)

Gate is $3 cheaper. Gate has slightly more treble extension and sub-bass slam. Zero is warmer overall. **Pick by treble preference**: more sparkle = Gate, smoother = Zero.

### vs. Tangzu Wan'er S.G. ($20)

Wan'er is slightly warmer than even the Zero. **Tighter race**: Zero is the safer default (more validated by community), Wan'er is the underdog pick.

### vs. KZ Castor Harman ($35)

Castor at $10 more has on-shell tuning switches (adjustability), better mid-bass control via dual-DD design. **Castor is the step up** if you want adjustability or have outgrown the Zero. [Read our Castor review](/reviews/kz-castor-review/).

### vs. Simgot EW200 ($40)

EW200 is 60% more expensive and **technically much better** — DLC diaphragm, more detail, more treble extension. **Skip the Zero if budget allows EW200** — it's a real upgrade.

## Verdict

**Pros**
- ✅ **The most validated sub-$30 IEM** — won't disappoint
- ✅ Warm-leaning tuning works for almost every genre
- ✅ Detachable 2-pin cable, future-proof
- ✅ Lightweight, comfortable for long sessions
- ✅ Smooth treble — never fatiguing
- ✅ Metal nozzle (rare at this price)

**Cons**
- ❌ Lacks treble sparkle (rolled-off above 10 kHz)
- ❌ Mids slightly less forward than the Chu 2 — vocals less prominent
- ❌ Only 3 sets of tips included
- ❌ Soundstage is intimate, not wide
- ❌ Newer competitors (Gate, Wan'er) have closed the gap

**Buy if:** you want the safest $25 IEM recommendation, you listen across many genres without strong preference, and you value cable swap-ability and smooth (non-fatiguing) sound.

**Skip if:** you want treble excitement (Gate or EW200), you prioritize vocal forwardness (Chu 2), or you have $40+ to spend on a clear technical upgrade (EW200, Castor).
