Simgot has spent the last few years building **the most thoughtful sub-$300 IEM lineup in ChiFi**. The EW200 ($40) brought DLC diaphragms to the budget tier. The EA500 brought tunable nozzles. The **EA1000 "Fermat" ($219)** is their statement piece — a single dynamic driver paired with a **passive radiator** for bass extension, plus three swappable tuning nozzles.

The name "Fermat" references Pierre de Fermat's Last Theorem — Simgot is making a math joke about driver topology. Whether the engineering joke lands depends on the listener; whether the IEM is worth $219 is what we'll address.

Short version: **the EA1000 is the most refined sub-$300 single-DD-with-PR IEM**. Premium build, three meaningful tuning options, technically excellent. Read on for who'd prefer it over the Truthear Nova or Tanchjim Origin.

## Specs at a glance

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Driver config** | Single 10mm DMDC™ Gen 2 dynamic + **passive radiator** |
| **Driver tech** | Dual Magnetic, Dual Chamber design + SDPGD™ diaphragm |
| **Impedance** | 16 Ω @ 1kHz |
| **Sensitivity** | 127 dB/mW |
| **Frequency response** | 10 Hz – 50 kHz |
| **Shell** | Stainless steel + glass faceplate |
| **Cable** | Detachable 0.78mm 2-pin, 3.5mm SE termination |
| **Tuning nozzles** | **3 included** — silver/red ring, silver/black ring, gold/white ring |
| **MSRP** | $219.99 |

The standout: **single DD + passive radiator** in a single IEM. The PR is a non-active driver that resonates from the DD's bass output, extending sub-bass without adding distortion. Same approach as the [Truthear Zero Red ($55)]({{< ref "guides/best-chifi-iems-for-bass-heads" >}}#1--truthear-zero-red-55--best-overall), but executed at flagship-tier quality.

## Who it's for

- **Single-DD purists** who want maximum bass extension without going hybrid
- **Tuning experimenters** — the three nozzles deliver real (not marketing) sound profile differences
- **Build enthusiasts** — stainless steel + glass at $219 is premium territory
- **Audiophiles between Aria 2 and Origin** — exactly the price gap

Skip it if:
- You want lush warm mids (Origin or Aria 2 better)
- You want max resolution (multi-BA hybrids like Nova have more detail)
- You're treble-sensitive (EA1000 leans slightly bright with all nozzles)

## Build, fit, accessories

**Stainless steel shell + glass faceplate** = serious build quality. Weight is noticeable (heavier than Moondrop Aria 2's zinc alloy) but the shape distributes well. The glass faceplate is gorgeous — chrome and glass refractions, looks like a luxury product. Some users will baby it; others should know **the glass is fragile if dropped**.

The **3 nozzles** are the headline interactive feature:
- **Silver / red ring** — Most balanced, the reference target
- **Silver / black ring** — Brighter treble, more sparkle (modern pop / EDM)
- **Gold / white ring** — Warmer mids, slightly recessed treble (jazz / classical)

Swapping nozzles takes 5 seconds (screw-on, screw-off). Differences are **audible enough to actually matter** — ~2-3 dB shifts in target bands.

Stock cable is high-quality braided, 3.5mm SE only. Modular plug not included (notable miss at $219).

## Sound (silver / red ring — reference)

The reference nozzle targets a **Harman-derived curve with elevated sub-bass** (the passive radiator's contribution). Mids are neutral, treble is bright but not harsh.

### Bass

The **passive radiator delivers sub-bass that single-DDs typically can't match**. Sub-bass extends cleanly to 25 Hz with audible texture and slam. Mid-bass is tight — kick drums punch without bloating into lower mids.

This is the EA1000's signature: **bass that feels physical without dominating**. EDM and hip-hop both work; rock kick-drums have weight.

### Mids

**Neutral, well-extracted, not particularly forward or recessed**. Vocals — male and female — sit at the right level. Guitars have body. Piano sustains cleanly.

The single-DD timbre is **more natural than a multi-BA IEM at this price** (Nova). Vocals don't have any "plasticky" character that BAs sometimes introduce. Slightly less lush than the Aria 2 — the EA1000 prioritizes accuracy over warmth.

### Treble

**The brightest single-DD treble under $300**. The SDPGD™ diaphragm has the speed for sharp transients — hi-hats glisten, cymbals decay with realistic shimmer. Detail retrieval is exceptional.

The trade-off: **upper-treble peak around 8 kHz** that **treble-sensitive listeners will find sharp**. Most listeners adapt; some won't. The warmth nozzle (gold/white) tames this for sensitive ears.

### Soundstage and imaging

**Wider stage than the Aria 2 or Origin**. Imaging is precise — instruments are clearly placed in 3D space. Approaches multi-driver IEM stage perception via single-DD coherence.

### Technical performance

**Top-tier for single-DD** in this price range. The DMDC™ Gen 2 driver delivers fast transients, low distortion, excellent dynamics. Detail retrieval beats the Tanchjim Origin's similarly-priced single-DD (different design philosophy — Origin prioritizes timbre, EA1000 prioritizes resolution).

## Source — easy to drive, scales

127 dB/mW sensitivity is **extremely efficient** — phone or dongle drives it loud easily. The EA1000 also **scales noticeably with cleaner sources** — a Moondrop Dawn Pro or FiiO JM21 makes the soundstage more 3D and bass more controlled.

Not as picky as the Letshuoer S12 Pro, but more rewarding to feed properly than the Aria 2.

## Nozzle comparison

| Nozzle | Sound | Best for |
|--------|-------|----------|
| **Silver / red ring** | Reference Harman + sub-bass | Most listeners default |
| **Silver / black ring** | Brighter, more treble sparkle | Pop, EDM, jazz cymbals |
| **Gold / white ring** | Warmer, less treble emphasis | Classical, ballads, vocal warmth, treble-sensitive |

**Pick the silver/red as your starting point**. Move to gold/white if treble bothers you, silver/black if you want more excitement.

## Verdict

**Pros**
- ✅ Passive radiator delivers sub-bass slam single-DDs can't match
- ✅ Three meaningful tuning nozzles (real differences)
- ✅ Premium stainless steel + glass build
- ✅ Excellent technical performance for single-DD
- ✅ Wide soundstage and precise imaging

**Cons**
- ❌ Stock cable is 3.5mm SE only (no modular plug at $219)
- ❌ Glass faceplate is fragile if dropped
- ❌ Treble can be sharp with silver nozzles (gold nozzle tames it)
- ❌ Heavier than typical aluminum/zinc IEMs

**Buy if:** you want a single-DD IEM with tuning flexibility and maximum sub-bass extension, you value premium build, and you're stepping up from sub-$150 IEMs.

**Skip if:** you want maximum resolution at this price (Truthear Nova has more BAs), you want warmer mid timbre (Tanchjim Origin or Moondrop Aria 2), or you need a modular cable.
