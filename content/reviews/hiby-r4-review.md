The FiiO JM21 ($200) set the bar for **best sub-$200 Android DAP**. The natural question: what does **double the budget** get you?

The **HiBy R4 at $399** is the answer most audiophile forums point to. Snapdragon 665 (better Android performance), dual ES9018C2M DACs, more refined sound, premium build. Whether it's worth the $200 premium over the JM21 depends entirely on what you'll actually notice.

Short version: **R4 is the polish step over JM21** — same idea, better execution. If you're already happy with the JM21, save the money. If you've outgrown it, R4 is the next step.

## Specs at a glance

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| **DAC chip** | **Dual ES9018C2M** (per channel) |
| **SoC** | **Qualcomm Snapdragon 665** (8-core, up to 2.0 GHz) |
| **OS** | Android 13 |
| **RAM** | **4 GB** (vs JM21's 3 GB) |
| **Storage** | 64 GB internal + microSD up to 2 TB |
| **Balanced output** | 4.4 mm, **600 mW** @ 32 Ω |
| **SE output** | 3.5 mm, ~250 mW @ 32 Ω |
| **SNR** | ~125 dB |
| **THD+N** | < 0.0008% |
| **Battery** | ~14 hours playback |
| **Display** | 4.7" 720×1280 with edge-to-edge glass |
| **Weight** | ~180 g |
| **MSRP** | $399 |

The standout upgrades from JM21: **Snapdragon 665 + 4 GB RAM** (better Android performance), **dual ES9018C2M** (instead of dual CS43198 — different sound character), **better build and screen**.

## Who it's for

- **JM21 owner** wanting the next step in resolution and Android responsiveness
- **Streaming user** who installs 3-4 apps and finds JM21's 3 GB RAM tight
- **Audiophile** who's decided DAP is primary listening device (not phone replacement)
- **Balanced-output enthusiasts** with demanding IEMs (S12 Pro, low-sensitivity planars)

Skip it if:
- You haven't maxed out the JM21 yet (no reason to spend 2x for marginal improvement)
- You want pure music DAP without Android (HiBy R3 II at $179 is more focused)
- You need flagship power (iBasso DX180 at $499 has 1000mW)

## Build, screen, ergonomics

The R4's **edge-to-edge glass display** is the immediate aesthetic upgrade. The screen is brighter, sharper, more color-accurate than the JM21's. Reading album art or browsing Tidal feels closer to a phone experience.

Aluminum frame, glass back — fingerprint-prone but premium-feeling. At 180 g it's heavier than the JM21 (165 g) and you feel it in a jeans pocket. Not pocket-uncomfortable but more "carry in hand for sessions" than "always-there".

Five physical buttons (volume up/down, play/pause, skip forward/back) feel solid. Volume rocker has good detent.

## Sound — dual ES9018C2M signature

The R4 uses **dual ES9018C2M DACs** (one per channel) — older but reference-grade ESS Sabre chips. **Different sound character than the JM21's Cirrus Logic CS43198**:

- **R4 (ES9018C2M)**: slightly more neutral, more analytical, more "ESS Sabre sparkle" on top
- **JM21 (CS43198)**: slightly warmer, smoother, more "Cirrus Logic naturalness"

In practical listening, the difference is **subtle**. ABX testing between the two requires specific tracks and very attentive listening. Most users won't reliably tell them apart in casual listening.

### What you'll notice immediately (vs JM21)

- **Slightly tighter bass control** under load (especially on planar IEMs)
- **Marginally cleaner mids** on complex passages
- **Better imaging precision** — instruments slightly more locked-in 3D
- **Lower noise floor** — quiet passages stay quieter

### What you won't notice

- Tonality difference is subtle, not transformational
- Detail retrieval is comparable
- Dynamic range is similar

**Honest take**: if you're listening to MP3s on a Moondrop Aria 2 from your couch, you won't hear the JM21→R4 upgrade. If you're listening to lossless FLAC on a Letshuoer S12 Pro through balanced cable on critical tracks, you might.

## Android performance

This is where the **R4 actually feels different from the JM21** day-to-day.

- **JM21 (3 GB RAM)**: boot ~30s, app switching has slight lag, FiiO recommends limiting to 1-2 streaming apps
- **R4 (4 GB RAM + Snapdragon 665)**: boot ~25s, app switching is smoother, you can run 3-4 streaming apps + FiiO Music without crashes

If you're a heavy streaming user (Spotify + Tidal + Apple Music + YouTube Music + Qobuz, picking by mood), the R4 makes that experience genuinely better.

## Battery and power

14 hours typical local playback. ~10 hours streaming over Wi-Fi. ~16h Bluetooth output mode (DAP-as-source).

The 600 mW balanced output is **enough for any reasonable IEM**. Drives the S12 Pro well (where JM21's 700 mW is slightly stronger but R4's cleaner output partly compensates). For demanding planar headphones, look at the iBasso DX180 (1000 mW) instead.

## Compared to

### vs. FiiO JM21 ($200)

The direct comparison. **JM21 is 50% cheaper**, slightly more output power (700 mW), warmer DAC tonality. **R4 is more refined overall** — better screen, better Android performance, slightly cleaner sound, more storage.

If you're shopping new, **JM21 is the better starting point**. R4 is the upgrade after you've maxed out JM21.

### vs. iBasso DX180 ($499)

DX180 is the next step up — **1000 mW balanced output**, similar Android performance, slightly more refined sound. **DX180 wins on output power for demanding headphones**; R4 is the value pick if pure IEM listening is your use case.

### vs. Shanling M3 Ultra ($549)

M3 Ultra has Shanling's warm signature. R4 has ESS Sabre's neutral character. **Pick by sound preference** — both are solid mid-tier DAPs.

### vs. FiiO M11 Plus ESS ($799)

M11 Plus ESS is flagship-tier — **dual ES9038PRO DACs, 8 GB RAM, premium build**. Worth the 2x price only if you've genuinely outgrown the R4. Most listeners won't.

## Verdict

**Pros**
- ✅ Genuine quality-of-life upgrade from JM21 (4 GB RAM + Snapdragon 665)
- ✅ Dual ES9018C2M deliver clean, neutral-bright sound
- ✅ Edge-to-edge glass screen feels phone-grade
- ✅ Better Android multitasking (run multiple streaming apps)
- ✅ Longer battery than JM21 (14h vs 12.5h)
- ✅ 64 GB internal storage (2x JM21's 32 GB)

**Cons**
- ❌ Slightly less output power than JM21 (600 mW vs 700 mW)
- ❌ Sound difference vs JM21 is subtle — hard to justify $200 premium for casual listeners
- ❌ Heavier than JM21 (180 g vs 165 g)
- ❌ Fingerprint-prone glass back

**Buy if:** you've maxed out the JM21 and want the polish step up, you stream heavily (3-4 apps), or you want a more premium build/screen experience.

**Skip if:** you don't already own and feel limited by the JM21 (just buy JM21 first), you need flagship output power (DX180), or you want pure music DAP without Android (R3 II).
