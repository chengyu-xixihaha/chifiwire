The original **Tin HiFi P2** earned its reputation in 2020 as the **planar IEM with technical chops** but also as the **IEM that destroyed sources** — its low sensitivity made it nearly impossible to drive from anything portable. Three years later, Tin HiFi released the **P2 Plus** — same 12mm planar driver, refined tuning, premium build, and (predictably) the same power requirements.

At $620, the P2 Plus competes with the [Letshuoer S12 Pro ($160)]({{< ref "reviews/letshuoer-s12-pro-review" >}}) — at 4x the price. Does it deliver 4x the experience? That's the question.

Short version: **the P2 Plus is the most refined planar IEM Tin HiFi has shipped**, but it's also one of the most demanding IEMs to drive at any price. Read on for who should care.

## Specs at a glance

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Driver** | Single 12mm planar magnetic |
| **Impedance** | 16 Ω (but **low sensitivity** — see below) |
| **Sensitivity** | **~98 dB/Vrms** (very low for an IEM) |
| **Frequency response** | 10 Hz – 40 kHz |
| **Shell** | CNC-machined aluminum, **gold-plated finish** |
| **Cable** | **Mogami 2381** (premium pro-audio cable), 0.78mm 2-pin, 3.5mm SE |
| **Tuning** | Refined neutral with corrections from original P2 |
| **MSRP** | $620 |

The standout: **Mogami 2381 cable** (a professional pro-audio cable normally $30+ alone) and **gold-plated finish** on the aluminum shell. The 12mm planar driver itself is identical to the original P2.

## Who it's for

- **Planar enthusiasts** who specifically prefer planar magnetic over other driver types
- **Desktop listeners** with proper headphone amps (FiiO K7, Topping A50s, or similar)
- **Tin HiFi fans** who appreciated the original P2 but wanted refinement
- **Studio/professional** users who need the technical performance + Mogami cable durability

Skip it if:
- You only listen from a phone or basic dongle (P2 Plus will sound thin and lifeless)
- You want the cheapest planar IEM (Letshuoer S12 Pro at $160 is the value pick)
- You want maximum sub-bass slam (S12 Pro has more, P2 Plus is more reference)
- You're treble-sensitive (P2 Plus retains some upper-treble energy)

## Build, fit, accessories

The P2 Plus's **gold-plated aluminum shell** is genuinely premium. CNC-machined, mirror-finish, weighty (heavier than typical IEMs). The build alone justifies a chunk of the price premium over budget planars.

The **Mogami 2381 cable** is the headline accessory. Mogami is a Japanese pro-audio cable manufacturer used in professional studio applications. It's durable, low microphonics, and feels reassuring in hand. Most $200+ IEMs ship with random Chinese cables; the P2 Plus shipping with Mogami is genuinely premium.

The 2-pin connectors are **recessed** (deep) — standard aftermarket cables fit but you'll need to verify the pin length.

**Accessories**: Spinfit ear tips (genuinely good — most IEMs ship generic tips), Tin HiFi instruction manual, warranty card, ear-wax filters, cleaning brush, tweezers for filter replacement, all in a separate cardboard accessory box. **Premium presentation**.

## Sound

The P2 Plus targets a **refined neutral signature** with corrections from the original P2's known flaws (which were primarily a slightly bright upper-treble that some found harsh).

### Bass

**Tight, controlled, fast sub-bass** with the planar's signature speed. Sub-bass extends to 20 Hz cleanly. Mid-bass is **less elevated than the S12 Pro** — more reference, less slam.

For bass-heavy genres (EDM, hip-hop), the **S12 Pro is more fun**. For accurate bass reproduction (classical, jazz with double-bass body), the P2 Plus is more correct.

### Mids

**The biggest refinement vs the original P2**. Mids are well-positioned, neither forward nor recessed. Vocals — male and female — have natural timbre, appropriate weight. Guitars have body without being colored.

The planar driver's speed manifests in mid-range transient accuracy — fingers on guitar strings are perceptibly faster/cleaner than dynamic-driver IEMs.

### Treble

**Refined from the original P2**. The original had upper-treble peaks that some found harsh; the P2 Plus tames this without losing extension. Cymbals shimmer; hi-hats have texture; brushed snares have detail without sibilance.

Still slightly **brighter than the Letshuoer S12 Pro** — closer to "reference accurate" than "smooth".

### Soundstage and imaging

**Wide stage with excellent depth**. Imaging is precise — instruments locked in 3D space. Approaching open-back headphone soundstage perception in IEM form.

### Technical performance

**Class-leading for IEMs**. Detail retrieval, transient response, dynamic range — all at flagship-tier levels. Resolution genuinely exceeds the S12 Pro (it should, at 4x the price).

## The power requirement — read this carefully

The P2 Plus is **one of the hardest-to-drive IEMs at any price**. With ~98 dB/Vrms sensitivity, you need:

- **NOT enough**: phone, basic Apple USB-C dongle, anything under 100 mW output
- **Minimum**: Moondrop Dawn Pro ($30) at full output — usable but thin
- **Recommended**: FiiO BTR7 ($199) balanced, FiiO JM21 ($200) balanced
- **Optimal**: Desktop amp — FiiO K7 (2W balanced), Topping A50s III, SMSL SH-9 v2

**If you don't have a real amp, do not buy the P2 Plus**. The driver simply won't develop bass and dynamic range without sufficient current. You'll think you got a defective unit when you really got an underpowered chain.

## Compared to

### vs. Letshuoer S12 Pro ($160)

The S12 Pro is **the value pick in planar IEMs**. P2 Plus has **slightly better refinement, premium build (Mogami cable + gold finish), wider stage**. But it costs 4x and **demands more power**.

Honest take: **most listeners should buy the S12 Pro and call it done**. The P2 Plus's refinement is real but the price gap is steeper than the audible gap.

[Read our S12 Pro review]({{< ref "reviews/letshuoer-s12-pro-review" >}}).

### vs. HiFiMan RE-2000 ($1,999)

HiFiMan flagship single-DD. Different driver philosophy — HiFiMan is dynamic, P2 Plus is planar. **HiFiMan wins on warmth; P2 Plus wins on detail and speed**.

### vs. 7Hz Timeless ($199)

The original budget planar darling. Cheaper, less refined, similar power requirements. **Timeless wins on value; P2 Plus is the upgrade path**.

### vs. Audeze Euclid ($1,299)

Audeze's closed-back planar IEM. **P2 Plus is more refined and Mogami cable beats Audeze's stock**. Different positioning (Euclid is closed-back) but similar driver tier.

## Verdict

**Pros**
- ✅ Refined neutral planar tuning (corrects original P2's brightness)
- ✅ Wide soundstage, excellent imaging, top-tier detail
- ✅ Premium build: CNC aluminum + gold-plated finish
- ✅ **Mogami 2381 cable** — pro-audio quality
- ✅ Spinfit tips included (most IEMs ship cheap tips)
- ✅ Detailed accessory presentation

**Cons**
- ❌ **Requires real amp** — 98 dB/Vrms sensitivity is brutal
- ❌ At $620, S12 Pro at $160 delivers 80% of the experience
- ❌ Less sub-bass slam than the S12 Pro (intentional, but worth noting)
- ❌ Heavier than typical IEMs (long-session comfort can suffer)

**Buy if:** you have a real amp (FiiO K7+ or equivalent), you prefer planar timbre over dynamic, and you want a refined flagship-tier planar IEM with premium build.

**Skip if:** you only have a phone or basic dongle (P2 Plus won't develop properly), you want the cheapest planar (S12 Pro), or you want maximum bass slam (different IEM category).
