Sennheiser launched the **Momentum 5 Wireless at $399.95** with one feature no other flagship has: a battery you can swap out yourself with a Phillips-head screwdriver. That alone has reviewers calling it the most honest premium headphone on the market. Whether it's the *best-sounding* flagship is a much messier question.

This roundup pulls together what Adam Birney and Dave Carr at SoundGuys and the long-form review at Headphones.com actually said, then lines the Momentum 5 up against the Sony WH-1000XM6, Bose QC Ultra Gen 2, and a few ChiFi planar over-ears most flagship coverage ignores.

## Verdict at a glance

- **Price:** $399.95 MSRP — undercuts Sony WH-1000XM6 ($449.99) and Bose QC Ultra Gen 2 ($449)
- **Battery:** ~57 hours ANC-on, **user-replaceable 700mAh cell** (4 screws, no service center)
- **ANC:** 4-mic array, "vastly better than Momentum 4"; Sony XM6 still wins on pure suppression
- **Sound:** Sub-bass heavier than Momentum 4, treble has edginess, **only 8-band graphic EQ** (no PEQ)
- **Codecs:** aptX Adaptive, AAC, SBC, LC3 — plus USB-C lossless audio
- **Weight/comfort:** Same chassis as Momentum 4 / HDB 630; ear cups still run small
- **Worth it:** Yes if you keep headphones 5+ years. Maybe not if you want best-in-class sound at this price

## What reviewers like

The headline almost every reviewer led with: **you can replace the battery yourself**. Adam Birney at SoundGuys went the furthest — his review is literally titled "the Momentum 5 makes every other flagship feel disposable." AirPods Max, Bose QC Ultra, and Sony's flagship over-ears all seal the cell. When that lithium-ion pack fades, you're either paying for a manufacturer repair or buying a new $400 product. The Momentum 5 needs four Phillips-head screws and a few minutes. Sennheiser even ships an in-app option to cap charging at 80% to extend cell life further.

The **57-hour battery** with ANC on is the longest of any flagship currently shipping — longer than the Sony XM6, the AirPods Max 2, and the Bose QC Ultra Gen 2. Combined with the replaceable cell, this is the part of the value story that nobody disputes.

ANC is the second big upgrade. Headphones.com was blunt about the Momentum 4: ANC fell short for a premium noise-canceling can. The Momentum 5 doubles the mic count from 2 to 4, and the result is "vastly better… genuinely competitive with class leaders like the Bose QC Ultra Gen 2 and Sony WH-1000XM6 (though I still give Sony the slight edge for pure ANC)." Birney concurs — Sennheiser is finally in the conversation, not chasing it.

Other consistent praise: the new carrying case is smaller and has a built-in handle (Headphones.com: "finally competitive with Bose for travel convenience"), **USB-C lossless audio** still beats Sony's XM6 omission in 2026, and the wind-cut filter and ambient mode both work. Dolby Atmos with head tracking is promised in a firmware update.

## Where it falls short

Sound is where the Momentum 5 stops being an obvious recommendation. Headphones.com put it cleanly: the Momentum 5 has "tons of sub-bass… actually even more than the Momentum 4 had." For some travelers that's the point. But the reviewer found it "overbearing and loose" compared to the **Sennheiser HDB 630** ($499), which uses tighter, more tonally-rich bass on the same chassis. Midrange and treble are "generally well-balanced and crisp" but with "moments of edginess and incoherence" the HDB 630 avoids.

The bigger frustration is EQ flexibility. The Momentum 5 ships with only an **8-band fixed graphic EQ** — no parametric EQ. The HDB 630, $100 more, gives you full PEQ. Sony's XM6 app gives you 10 bands. If the stock tuning doesn't fit your head, you have limited tools to fix it. Dave Carr at SoundGuys also notes Sony bundles **DSEE Extreme** for restoring detail to compressed files, which Sennheiser doesn't match.

Comfort gets a mixed read. The chassis is shared with the Momentum 4 and HDB 630, which means the **ear cups still run small** — Headphones.com noted ears touching the inside of the baffle. The headband also lacks a centering mechanism, so it's easy to wear them slightly off-axis without noticing. On a $400 headphone two generations in, neither should still be on the list.

Birney's whole framing is *durability*, not *sound*. He doesn't claim the Momentum 5 sounds better than its rivals — he claims it'll still be in service when its rivals are bricks. That's the cleanest summary of its actual value proposition.

## How it compares (Sony / B&W / Bose)

**Sony WH-1000XM6 ($449.99)** — Still the benchmark. Carr is direct: "Sony still has the edge." Stronger ANC, more powerful app, 10-band EQ vs Sennheiser's 8, DSEE Extreme. Sony also drops to street price fast; the Momentum 5 will hold MSRP longer. The Momentum 5 wins on **battery life, USB-C audio, and replaceable cell**. If those three matter, take the Sennheiser.

**Bose QC Ultra Gen 2 ($449)** — Headphones.com called the fit "potentially more comfortable" and singled out Bose's CustomTune, which measures how the headphone actually performs on your specific head and corrects for it. ANC is roughly comparable to the Momentum 5. If you've struggled with headphone fit, Bose is the safer buy.

**B&W Px8 (~$549-$699)** — Worth flagging if you want a warmer, more refined tonality and don't care about top-tier ANC. The Momentum 5 is a more practical traveler; B&W is a more musical headphone with weaker noise cancelling. For the newer S2 generation specifically, see our [Sony 1000X The Collexion vs B&W PX8 S2 head-to-head]({{< ref "/vs/sony-1000x-collexion-vs-bowers-wilkins-px8-s2" >}}).

**Sennheiser HDB 630 ($499)** — Headphones.com kept circling back to this one. Same chassis, better stock tuning, full parametric EQ, better dongle. If sound quality is your priority and you can find the extra $100, this is the in-house upsell. If you want wired studio reference instead of wireless travel, our [Sennheiser HD 480 Pro review]({{< ref "/reviews/sennheiser-hd-480-pro-review" >}}) covers the new $399 closed-back contender from the same brand.

**JBL Tour One M3 ($399.99)** — Same price, much more feature-loaded. Carr's pick if you want LDAC, Auracast, the SMART Tx transmitter, and a 12-band EQ. Loses on the replaceable battery.

**Sennheiser Momentum 4 (~$229 street)** — The dark horse. Carr's argument: at street price you save ~$170 and give up "surprisingly little" — over 56 hours of battery, USB-C audio, multipoint, aptX HD and aptX Adaptive. Sound out of the box also tracks closer to SoundGuys' preference curve.

### The ChiFi-Wire angle: planar over-ears worth knowing about

The Momentum 5 is a wireless ANC headphone, so it doesn't compete directly with wired audiophile cans. But if you're at a desk most of the time and don't strictly need ANC, the same $400 buys very different things in ChiFi land:

- **Moondrop Cosmo** (~$450) — open-back planar, Harman-leaning tuning, wired-only. Raw fidelity over practical features.
- **FiiO FT5** (~$449) — planar over-ear, swappable pads, wired-only. The "detail over convenience" pick.
- **Edifier Stax Spirit S5** (~$499) — wireless planar with LDAC and Snapdragon Sound, no ANC, ~80h battery. The option flagship roundups always skip.

None of these replace the Momentum 5 on a plane. All three out-resolve it at a desk.

## Who should buy it

- **Frequent travelers** who want the longest ANC battery on the market and a case that fits in a backpack
- **People who keep headphones 5+ years** — the replaceable battery is a real multi-year cost saving
- **Bass-forward listeners** who actively want more sub-bass than the Momentum 4 delivered
- **Anyone who needs USB-C lossless audio at a desk and Bluetooth on the go**, without owning two pairs

## Who should skip it

- **Sound-quality maxers** — spend $100 more on the HDB 630 (PEQ, tighter tuning) or grab a planar like the Edifier Stax Spirit S5
- **Best-in-class ANC seekers** — the Sony WH-1000XM6 still wins by a hair and drops to street price faster
- **Small-cup-sensitive listeners** — the chassis hasn't changed; if the Momentum 4 didn't fit, the Momentum 5 won't either
- **Deal hunters** — the Momentum 4 at ~$229 street is the better buy until the Momentum 5 sees its first real discount
- **Aggressive EQ users** — the 8-band graphic EQ is limiting vs Sony's 10-band or the HDB 630's PEQ

The Momentum 5 isn't the best-sounding $400 headphone. It might be the best-engineered one. Whether that trade lands depends entirely on how long you plan to own it.
