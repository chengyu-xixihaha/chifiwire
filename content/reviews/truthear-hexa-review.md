If you've spent any time on r/iems in 2024-2026, you've seen the **Truthear Hexa recommendation reflex**. Someone asks "best IEM under $100" — within an hour, three different commenters reply "get the Hexa". This review explains why that reflex exists, what the Hexa actually does well (and where it doesn't), and who should buy one in 2026 vs the [newer Pure]({{< ref "reviews/truthear-pure-review" >}}).

**Short version**: the Hexa is **the analytical, detail-forward sub-$100 hybrid that set the modern ChiFi sub-$100 standard**. Three years on, nothing has truly replaced it — sequels like the Pure and competitors like Aria 2 carve out different niches without dethroning the Hexa for technical performance per dollar.

## Specs at a glance

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Driver config** | 1 DD + 3 BA hybrid |
| **DD** | 10mm LCP (liquid crystal polymer) diaphragm |
| **BAs** | 3× custom balanced armatures (1 mid, 2 treble) |
| **Impedance** | 20 Ω |
| **Sensitivity** | 120 dB/Vrms @ 1kHz |
| **Frequency response** | 20 Hz – 20 kHz |
| **Shell** | Resin body with metal nozzle, 3D-printed |
| **Cable** | 0.78mm 2-pin detachable, 3.5mm SE |
| **Tuning** | In-house Harman-adjacent target |
| **MSRP** | $78.99 |

The Hexa uses a **straightforward tribrid design**: dynamic driver for sub-bass and lower mids, then three BAs cover mids and treble. No crossover wizardry, no marketing-driven driver count inflation — the engineering serves the sound, not the spec sheet.

## Who it's for

- **First-time audiophile** wanting one IEM that does everything reasonably well
- **Detail-focused listener** — Hexa has class-leading resolution at this tier
- **EDM / electronic / metal** — sub-bass extension + fast transients
- **Phone-only listener** who doesn't have a DAP — 20 Ω + 120 dB is phone-friendly
- **Reference seeker** on a budget — the Hexa is the closest sub-$100 IEM to neutral

Skip it if:
- You want **warm, lush, vocal-flattering tone** → [Pure]({{< ref "reviews/truthear-pure-review" >}}) or Moondrop Aria 2
- You're treble-sensitive — Hexa's BAs deliver real treble extension
- You exclusively listen to acoustic / classical — planar [S12 Pro]({{< ref "reviews/letshuoer-s12-pro-review" >}}) sounds more "real"
- You want maximum sub-bass slam — [Truthear Zero Red]({{< ref "reviews/truthear-zero-red-review" >}}) has more

## Sound impressions

### Sub-bass

The Hexa's LCP DD delivers **clean, controlled sub-bass extension**. It rolls flat to 30 Hz with surprising authority — most sub-$100 IEMs lose extension here, the Hexa doesn't. The trade-off: this is **clean** sub-bass, not "slam". For warm bass-heads, look at Zero Red.

### Mid-bass

Where Hexa shines for analytical listeners: **mid-bass is tucked, not bloated**. Kick drums hit but don't blur into the mids. For modern production where mid-bass is often overcooked, the Hexa's restraint is refreshing.

### Mids

**Slightly recessed**. This is the Hexa's most-debated characteristic. Vocals sit half a step back in the mix; instruments around vocals (rhythm guitars, piano) get equal billing. The 3 BAs deliver excellent transient detail, but the staging puts you 5-6 rows back, not in row 1.

For warm vocal music, this can feel **emotionally distant**. For complex arrangements (orchestral, layered electronic, prog rock), it lets you hear every layer.

### Treble

**Extended and slightly forward**. The BA tuning gives you cymbals, high-hats, breath sounds, finger slides on strings — all the "audiophile" sparkle markers. Some listeners describe Hexa treble as "lit up" — others call it perfectly judged. **Depends on your treble preference**.

If you're new to BA treble, expect 1-2 weeks of acclimatization. After that, the analytical detail becomes part of why you love the IEM.

### Soundstage and imaging

**Wider than most sub-$100 IEMs** but not vast. Stereo imaging is precise — instruments occupy distinct, stable positions. Depth is moderate — Hexa stages sound "spread laterally" more than "front-to-back".

### Speed and dynamics

**Fast**. The BAs handle transients quickly; the LCP DD doesn't smear. Complex passages (death metal blast beats, busy orchestral, layered EDM) stay coherent. This is where the Hexa beats most single-DD competitors at this tier.

## Build and fit

- **Resin shell** — light, doesn't pressure-build in ear like metal IEMs
- **Standard 0.78mm 2-pin** — cable swaps easy
- **Cable included is acceptable** — not premium but workable
- **Nozzle size is large** — some small-ear users find them just-too-big

The shell shape is **ergonomic for medium-to-large ear shapes**. Small-ear users may want to test fit before buying. See our [small ears guide]({{< ref "guides/best-chifi-iems-for-small-ears" >}}) for alternatives.

## Source pairing

- **Phone-only**: works fine, won't shine
- **Dongle DAC** (Fosi, FiiO KA17, Apple dongle): meaningful upgrade — more dynamic range
- **DAP** ([FiiO JM21]({{< ref "reviews/fiio-jm21-review" >}}), [HiBy R4]({{< ref "reviews/hiby-r4-review" >}})): the Hexa scales further than its price suggests

A good source unlocks the Hexa's resolution. Don't pair with low-quality dongles or you're leaving 30% of the performance on the table.

## Where it falls short

- **Vocals can feel slightly distant** — if vocals are your primary focus, Pure or Aria 2 are better
- **Treble is real treble** — if you're treble-sensitive, EQ down 7-9 kHz or pick warm alternatives
- **Bass slam is moderate** — clean over impactful; bass-heads want [Zero Red]({{< ref "reviews/truthear-zero-red-review" >}}) instead
- **Shell can be a tight fit** for small ears (check [small ears guide]({{< ref "guides/best-chifi-iems-for-small-ears" >}}))
- **No 4.4mm balanced cable included** — pure SE; balanced cable is extra

## Verdict — should you buy the Hexa in 2026?

**Yes — if you're new to ChiFi and want one IEM that does most things excellently** at $79.

**Yes — if you're an audiophile collector looking for the "neutral reference" sub-$100 anchor** to compare warm IEMs against.

**Maybe — if you've already heard Hexa and want to upgrade**. The Pure is the warm sibling; the Nova is the [next-step Harman benchmark at $150]({{< ref "reviews/truthear-nova-review" >}}); for planar fans, the [S12 Pro]({{< ref "reviews/letshuoer-s12-pro-review" >}}) is the natural progression.

**No — if you're treble-sensitive or want vocal-led tuning**. Pure or Aria 2 are better choices.
