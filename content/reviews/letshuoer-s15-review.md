The [Letshuoer S12 Pro ($160)]({{< ref "reviews/letshuoer-s12-pro-review" >}}) defined "budget planar IEM" for two years. The **S15 at $329** is the third-generation successor — same 14.8mm driver concept, refined R-Sonic passive filtering, new lightweight 3D-printed shell. The question every S12 Pro owner asks: **is it worth the 2x price jump?**

Short version: **the S15 is technically more refined but the audible upgrade is smaller than the price gap suggests**. Read on for who'd justify it.

## Specs at a glance

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Driver** | **Third-generation 14.8mm dual-chamber planar magnetic** |
| **Filtering** | 6mm R-Sonic passive filtering module + dual acoustic tube |
| **Impedance** | 30 Ω |
| **Sensitivity** | ~106 dB/Vrms (slightly higher than S12 Pro) |
| **Shell** | Lightweight 3D-printed (HeyGears) — blue body + grey CNC aluminum faceplate |
| **Cable** | 216-strand silver-plated mono-crystalline copper, **3.5/2.5/4.4mm modular** |
| **Tuning** | Relaxed, low in-ear pressure, planar-typical V-shape (less aggressive than S12 Pro) |
| **MSRP** | $329 |

The headline upgrades over S12 Pro:
- **Third-generation planar driver** (refined diaphragm + magnet array)
- **R-Sonic passive filtering** — Letshuoer's proprietary acoustic filter that tames driver resonances
- **3D-printed shell from HeyGears** (premium ear-mold company)
- **Higher sensitivity** — slightly easier to drive than the notoriously power-hungry S12 Pro

## Who it's for

- **S12 Pro owner** who specifically wants the next-generation refinement
- **Listener moving up from sub-$200 planars** seeking premium build
- **Comfort-prioritized listeners** — S15's lightweight 3D-printed shell is significantly more comfortable than the S12 Pro's metal
- **Modular-cable users** with 2.5mm balanced sources (S15 includes 2.5mm plug, S12 Pro doesn't include 2.5)

Skip it if:
- You already own the S12 Pro and like its sound (incremental upgrade)
- You can't justify $169 premium over S12 Pro
- You want maximum slam (S12 Pro is more aggressive)
- You're not committed to planar driver type

## Build, fit, accessories

The **3D-printed HeyGears shell** is the standout build feature. HeyGears is a premium ear-molding company that does custom IEMs for several boutique brands; Letshuoer using their tech here means the universal-fit shape is **engineered for ergonomics** rather than just "looks pretty".

Real-world: **the S15 fits more comfortably than the S12 Pro for most ears**. The aluminum-faceplate-on-resin construction is **lighter than fully-metal alternatives** — usable for 4+ hour sessions where the S12 Pro starts feeling heavy.

The **216-strand SPC cable** is genuinely premium — feels like a $50 aftermarket cable, low microphonics, supple. The **modular plug system includes 2.5mm, 3.5mm, AND 4.4mm** — most $300+ IEMs include only 3.5 + 4.4. The 2.5mm is useful for older Astell&Kern or Hiby DAPs.

90-degree angle connectors at the source end fit DAP pockets better than straight plugs.

Accessory package is generous: multiple tip types, leather case, cable + 3 plugs, cleaning tools.

## Sound

The S15 targets a **slightly warmer, more relaxed version of the S12 Pro's tuning**. The R-Sonic filtering tames the upper-treble peaks that some found fatiguing on the S12 Pro.

### Bass

**Deep sub-bass extension** — the planar driver's signature. The R-Sonic filter slightly **tames mid-bass slam** compared to S12 Pro — bass is cleaner and more controlled, but **less "fun"**. Some listeners find this a downgrade.

If you loved the S12 Pro's bass authority, you might find the S15 too polite. If you found S12 Pro's bass overcooked, the S15 nails it.

### Mids

**More forward and refined than S12 Pro**. The new driver + filtering combination brings mids slightly out of the V-shape recession. Vocals are clearer, instruments have more presence.

Still **not as natural as single-DD timbre** ([Tanchjim Origin]({{< ref "reviews/tanchjim-origin-review" >}})) — planar mids retain a slight "fast/clinical" character. But meaningfully better than S12 Pro on midrange.

### Treble

**Smoother and less aggressive than S12 Pro**. The R-Sonic filter tames the 7-8 kHz peak that bothered treble-sensitive listeners on the S12 Pro. Cymbals and hi-hats are present without the bite.

**Trade-off**: lost some excitement. The S12 Pro's bright treble was part of its character — S15 is more "audiophile correct" but less "energetic".

### Soundstage and imaging

**Wider stage than S12 Pro**. The 3D-printed shell shape and refined driver give better spatial perception. Imaging is precise. Approaching open-back headphone stage perception.

### Technical performance

**Refined improvements**. Detail retrieval is slightly better. Transient response is similar (both are planar — fast). Resolution is more accurate. The S15 doesn't suddenly become a different class of IEM — **it's a polished S12 Pro at 2x the price**.

## Source — still needs a real chain

At 106 dB sensitivity, the S15 is **slightly easier to drive than S12 Pro** (102 dB) but still demands a real source. Phone alone underdrives it; you need:

- **Minimum**: Moondrop Dawn Pro ($30 dongle DAC) at full balanced output
- **Recommended**: [FiiO JM21]({{< ref "reviews/fiio-jm21-review" >}}) ($200) or [HiBy R4]({{< ref "reviews/hiby-r4-review" >}}) ($399) DAPs
- **Optimal**: Desktop amp like FiiO K7 or Topping A50s

The S15's improvements over S12 Pro are **most audible on properly-powered sources**. Underpowered, you might not hear the upgrade at all.

## Compared to

### vs. Letshuoer S12 Pro ($160) — the direct comparison

Honest take: **most S12 Pro owners shouldn't upgrade**. The S15 is technically better in ~7 ways but the audible improvement is incremental, not transformational. Save the $169 and put it toward your source chain instead.

**When the S15 IS worth it**:
- You found S12 Pro's metal shell uncomfortable (S15's 3D-printed shell wins on comfort)
- You're treble-sensitive and disliked S12 Pro's upper-treble peak
- You have 2.5mm balanced source (S15 includes that plug)
- You want maximum refinement at sub-$500

## Verdict

**Pros**
- ✅ Third-gen 14.8mm planar driver with R-Sonic filtering
- ✅ Lightweight 3D-printed HeyGears shell — comfortable for long sessions
- ✅ Premium 216-strand SPC cable with 3-plug modular system (2.5/3.5/4.4)
- ✅ Smoother treble than S12 Pro (no aggressive peaks)
- ✅ Slightly easier to drive than S12 Pro
- ✅ Wider soundstage and more refined imaging

**Cons**
- ❌ **2x the price of S12 Pro for incremental improvement** — questionable value
- ❌ Less bass slam than S12 Pro (some will see this as downgrade)
- ❌ Still needs a real source — not phone-friendly
- ❌ Resin body less premium-feeling than fully-metal flagships at this price

**Buy if:** you have a real source chain, you want planar refinement, you found S12 Pro uncomfortable or too aggressive in treble.

**Skip if:** you own and like the S12 Pro (don't upgrade), you can't justify $329 over $160, you don't have a proper DAC/amp setup.
