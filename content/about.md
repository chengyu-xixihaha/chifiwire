ChiFi Wire is an independent audio publication covering Chinese hi-fi gear — IEMs, headphones, DAPs, DACs, and amplifiers — for English-speaking audiophiles worldwide.

"ChiFi" (Chinese hi-fi) has gone from a Reddit curiosity to a legitimate segment of the audio market. Brands like Moondrop, Truthear, Letshuoer, FiiO, and Simgot now produce IEMs that rival products costing two to five times more from legacy Western brands. The problem is that most of the best information about this gear lives in scattered Reddit threads, Discord servers, and reviews written for audiences who already know the vocabulary.

ChiFi Wire exists to fix that. We write for listeners who want clear, honest, jargon-light guidance — whether you're spending $25 on your first real IEM or $300 on a flagship tribrid.

## What we cover

- **In-depth reviews** — each product gets extended listening across multiple genres before we write a single word. We cover sound character, fit, build quality, accessories, source requirements, and value within the price tier.
- **Buying guides** — picks organized by budget, use case, and sound preference. We tell you which one to buy, not just which ones exist.
- **Head-to-head comparisons** — direct matchups of competing IEMs at the same price tier. The most common question in ChiFi is "which one should I get?" — we answer it.
- **Brand overviews** — complete lineup guides for brands like Moondrop, Truthear, and 7Hz, so you can navigate a full product range without reading a dozen separate reviews.

## Our review process

Every review on ChiFi Wire follows the same process:

1. **Extended listening period** — minimum two weeks of daily use across varied genres: electronic, jazz, acoustic, classical, hip-hop, and rock. No first-impression hot takes.
2. **Source testing** — we test with phone output, a basic dongle DAC, and a dedicated source where relevant. Source-sensitivity is always noted.
3. **Tip rolling** — stock tips and at least two aftermarket options (wide-bore and narrow-bore) to find the best pairing.
4. **Comparison context** — every reviewed product is placed in context against two to four alternatives at the same price tier.
5. **Pricing verification** — prices are checked at publication and reflect street price, not inflated MSRP where manufacturers discount immediately.

## Editorial independence

ChiFi Wire is reader-supported through affiliate commissions. When you buy through our links (to Linsoul, HiFiGo, Apos Audio, Amazon, and others), we may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. This is how the site stays funded.

We do not accept free products in exchange for reviews, sponsored placements, or paid rankings. Every recommendation reflects our honest assessment. If a product is mediocre, we say so — even if it's popular.

Our affiliate relationships are disclosed on every page that contains affiliate links. Full details are on our [Affiliate Disclosure](/affiliate-disclosure/) page.

## About the ratings

Our ratings run from 1 to 5 and reflect **value within the price tier**, not absolute performance. A $25 IEM rated 4.5 is exceptional for $25 — not competitive with a $300 tribrid. Context always matters more than the number.

- **5.0** — benchmark product; best in class for the price, no meaningful competition
- **4.5–4.9** — excellent, minor trade-offs only
- **4.0–4.4** — very good, some compromises worth knowing
- **3.5–3.9** — decent, specific strengths but notable weaknesses
- **Below 3.5** — only recommended for niche use cases
