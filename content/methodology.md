ChiFi Wire reviews try to be **specific, falsifiable, and useful for buying decisions**. This page explains how we get there.

## Review process

Every product review on ChiFi Wire follows the same structure:

1. **Specs at a glance** — driver config, impedance, sensitivity, included accessories
2. **Who it's for** — the use cases where this product makes sense
3. **Sound impressions** — bass, mids, treble, soundstage, speed, broken down separately
4. **Source pairing** — what dongle / DAP / amp gets the most out of it
5. **Versus competitors** — direct comparison to similar gear at the same price tier
6. **Where it falls short** — every product has compromises; we name them
7. **Verdict + rating** — 1-5 stars, with reasoning

No "five star everything" — if a product compromises on something, the rating reflects it.

## Listening setup

Reviews are conducted with a consistent reference source chain to minimize variables:

- **Mid-tier dongle DAC**: Fosi DS2 ($40) — covers most IEM listening
- **High-power dongle**: FiiO KA17 ($90) — for demanding planar IEMs
- **DAP**: HiBy R4 — for genuine portable audiophile use cases
- **Desktop**: Topping E50 II → Topping A50s amp → various headphones

We listen on each product **for 20-40 hours minimum** before publishing. Brief impressions from 1-2 hour sessions get filed as "first impressions" and labeled as such, not full reviews.

## Music library used

Tracks span genres to avoid taste-bias:

- **Electronic / EDM**: Aphex Twin, Boards of Canada, Bonobo, Tycho
- **Hip-hop**: Kendrick Lamar, Tyler the Creator, Run the Jewels
- **Rock**: Pink Floyd, Tool, Radiohead, Mastodon
- **Jazz**: Miles Davis, John Coltrane, Brad Mehldau
- **Classical**: Mahler, Stravinsky, Brahms, modern composers
- **Vocal-led**: Adele, Bon Iver, Sufjan Stevens
- **Acoustic**: Iron & Wine, Joni Mitchell, jazz quartets

Reviewers describe **specific tracks** when calling out particular characteristics (e.g., "Tracks like ___ reveal a treble peak around 7 kHz").

## Measurement caveats

We **don't have a calibrated coupler** (711, B&K 5128, etc.). Reviews are subjective listening impressions, not lab measurements. When we reference frequency response patterns, we cite measurement-focused reviewers (Crinacle, Headphones.com, Super Reviews) who have proper rigs.

This is a limitation. We compensate by:
- Cross-referencing multiple measurement sources
- Distinguishing "what I hear" from "what measurements show"
- Avoiding fabricated dB numbers

## Editorial principles

- **No manufacturer payola**: we don't accept free products in exchange for positive coverage. Loaners are clearly disclosed.
- **Affiliate disclosure**: we may earn commissions through affiliate links, **at no extra cost to you**. See our [Affiliate Disclosure](/affiliate-disclosure/).
- **Specs from primary sources**: where possible, specs come from manufacturer documentation, not aggregator sites.
- **Conflict-of-interest disclosure**: if a reviewer owns a competing product, we say so.

## Update policy

- Reviews are **timestamped** with both `date` (originally published) and `lastmod` (last reviewed)
- Material errors are corrected and noted at the bottom of the review with a date stamp
- When new information emerges (firmware updates, retuned versions, recalled units), we add follow-up notes

## Topic coverage

We cover:
- **IEMs** ($20 to $2,000+ tier)
- **Headphones** (open-back, closed-back; planar magnetic, dynamic, electrostatic)
- **DAPs** (Digital Audio Players)
- **DACs and amps** (desktop and portable)
- **Cables and tips** (rarely — only when audibly impactful)

We don't cover:
- Bluetooth-only audio (different evaluation criteria, not our focus)
- Speakers (different domain expertise needed)
- Studio monitoring / pro audio (different use case)

## About the team

ChiFi Wire is an editorial project. Reviews are written by a small editorial team with combined audiophile experience spanning **15+ years across IEMs, headphones, DAPs, and source gear**. We aren't trying to be the biggest review site — we're trying to be the most useful sub-$1,000 buying-decision resource for the global ChiFi market.

For technical depth on measurements, we defer to:
- **[Crinacle](https://crinacle.com/)** — the canonical IEM measurement database
- **[Headphones.com Reviews](https://headphones.com/blogs/reviews)** — industry-leading independent reviews
- **[Super Reviews](https://www.youtube.com/@SuperReview)** — DougMontalbanoChannel sound impressions
- **[Resolve at The Headphone Show](https://www.youtube.com/@HeadphonesShow)** — deep technical analysis

We complement those with:
- Use-case organization (rather than measurement-first)
- Reddit consensus tracking
- Buying decision support (vs technical write-ups)
- Source-chain context
