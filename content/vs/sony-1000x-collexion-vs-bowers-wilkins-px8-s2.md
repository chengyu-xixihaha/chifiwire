If you're shopping luxury wireless ANC headphones in 2026, two names dominate the $700-plus tier: **Sony 1000X "The Collexion" ($649.95)** and **Bowers & Wilkins PX8 S2 ($799.99)**. Both are leather-clad, metal-skeleton flagships with 24+ hour battery. Both weigh over 300g.

But they're **not the same product** — one is a feature-loaded modern flagship, the other is a retro-styled luxury object with audio personality. **Which should YOU buy?**

## TL;DR

- **Sony 1000X The Collexion**: better ANC, more neutral tuning, modern app, LE Audio, LDAC, $150 cheaper
- **B&W PX8 S2**: prettier design, USB-C lossless wired, physical buttons, aptX Adaptive — but worse tuning and weaker app

For **most buyers**: **Sony 1000X The Collexion** wins on price, ANC, sound score, and feature depth.
For **lossless wired listening or pure aesthetics**: **PX8 S2** — but only if you find it discounted.

## Specs side by side

| | **Sony 1000X The Collexion** | **B&W PX8 S2** |
|---|--------------------|---------------------|
| **MSRP** | $649.95 | $799.99 |
| **Bluetooth** | 6.0 | 5.3 |
| **Codecs** | SBC, LC3, AAC, LDAC | SBC, AAC, aptX, aptX Adaptive |
| **LE Audio** | Yes | No |
| **Wired** | Analog only (3.5mm) | Analog + USB-C digital |
| **Weight** | 320g | 310g |
| **IP rating** | None | None |

| **Battery** | 24+ hours | 24+ hours |

The B&W is 10g lighter on paper but feels heavier in practice due to clamp and pad geometry. Both lack any IP rating, which is genuinely disappointing at this price.

## Price & build

**Sony is $150 cheaper out of the gate.** Both use metal skeletons with leather-like cladding and premium carrying cases. But the **PX8 S2 leans into a deliberately retro aesthetic** — flashier, more "old-money headphone" energy. The Sony is more understated luxury, closer to a refined WH-1000XM6 than a fashion statement.

If you want headphones as accessory, B&W wins on visual presence. If you want something practical to live with daily — easier controls, better case, better app — Sony wins. For a $150 premium, the PX8 S2 needs to justify itself. Spoiler: it mostly doesn't.

## Sound signature

**Sony wins for more listeners. PX8 S2 polarizes.**

Run both through HEAD acoustics' Multi-Dimensional Audio Quality Scores algorithm with a 200-person sample and you get: **Sony 4.5 MOS ("good") vs B&W 3.4 MOS ("okay")**. That's not a small gap.

What's happening tonally:

- **Sony 1000X The Collexion**: fairly neutral with a **+4-6 dB bass shelf below 600 Hz**. Warm but not bloated. Bass-lovers love it; the rest can drop it 3 dB in the 10-band EQ and have a nearly reference-tuned wireless flagship.
- **B&W PX8 S2**: more "editorialized." Mids are fine, but the **lows are wobbly** and there's a **big peak at 8-9 kHz** that gets fatiguing on busy recordings. The B&W app only ships a **5-band EQ** — not enough resolution to surgically fix that spike.

The Sony is easier to "rescue" with the app. If you want V-shaped energy at both ends, the PX8 S2 might genuinely appeal. Otherwise, Sony.

## ANC & call quality

**ANC: Sony wins clearly.** The Collexion has both better **passive isolation** AND more **active cancellation** on top. PX8 S2's ANC is improved over older B&W cans, but not in the same league as Sony's flagship pipeline. For commuting, flights, or noisy offices: get the Sony.

**Call quality: PX8 S2 slightly edges out, with a caveat.** In **wind noise**, the PX8 S2's mic handles outdoor callers a touch better. But its mic is bassier and can sound odd in ideal conditions. The Sony handles reverberant rooms, office chatter, and street noise with strong rejection.

Net: if you take **a lot of windy outdoor calls**, PX8 S2. Otherwise basically a wash with a slight Sony lean for indoor/office.

## Comfort & battery

Both are **over 300g**, and that's the dominant story. Glasses-wearers will feel both on long sessions.

- **Sony Collexion (320g)**: stays planted because touch gestures don't require press force. Deep, well-shaped pads.
- **PX8 S2 (310g)**: 10g lighter but **physical buttons require pressing into a cup that's already clamping your head** — fatiguing over hours.

Neither is great for all-day wear, but **Sony is the lesser fatigue option**. Want comfortable luxury? Look at B&W PX7 S3 ($449) — plasticky but genuinely comfy.

**Battery**: both exceed 24 hours. Non-factor.

## Codec & app

This is where the gap widens.

**Codec support:**
- Sony: SBC, LC3, AAC, **LDAC**, plus **LE Audio**
- B&W: SBC, AAC, aptX, **aptX Adaptive**

LDAC vs aptX Adaptive is a wash for quality, but Sony's **LE Audio support is a real edge** for latency-sensitive use (video, games). The B&W is stuck on BT 5.3 with no LE Audio.

**Wired:** Sony is analog-only. B&W does analog AND **USB-C digital** for lossless. **This is the one place PX8 S2 wins decisively** — only the B&W does true lossless wired from a phone or DAC.

**App:** Sony's Sound Connect is feature-loaded — 10-band EQ, spatial audio, Find My Device, ANC modes, Multipoint, listening profiles. B&W's app is bare-bones — 5-band EQ and basic settings, no spatial audio. Sony's ecosystem is a generation ahead.

## Who should pick which

### Get **Sony 1000X The Collexion** if you:
- Want **best-in-class ANC** for commuting or flights
- Care about a **modern, neutral-leaning tuning** that's easy to EQ
- Want **LDAC + LE Audio** for low-latency video/games
- Prefer **touch controls** over button-mashing into your skull
- Want the **better app and 10-band EQ**
- Want to **save $150**
- Are upgrading from a WH-1000XM5/M6 and want the same DNA in a premium chassis

### Get **B&W PX8 S2** if you:
- Want the **prettier, more retro luxury aesthetic**
- Need **USB-C lossless wired listening**
- Prefer **physical buttons** (rain, gloves, no false touches)
- Genuinely like a **V-shaped tuning with hot 8-9 kHz energy**
- Take a lot of **outdoor calls in wind**
- Find it on **significant sale** ($600 or less)
- Don't need killer ANC

## Bottom line

For **most buyers**, **Sony 1000X The Collexion wins decisively**: better ANC, better tuning to more ears, better app, modern codecs, LE Audio, and **$150 cheaper**. It's also easier to live with daily — controls don't fight you, and the 10-band EQ actually has enough resolution to fix what's wrong.

**PX8 S2 is for a specific buyer**: values design and aesthetics as much as sound, wants **USB-C lossless wired**, and either likes the V-shape or doesn't mind a 5-band EQ. At MSRP, hard to recommend over the Sony. **Below $650 on sale, it becomes interesting.**

Reality check: **Sony WH-1000XM6 ($448)** and **B&W PX7 S3 ($449)** deliver most of what these flagships do for $200-350 less. The Collexion and PX8 S2 are **luxury purchases, not value picks**. Buy them for the chassis, case, and feel — not because they sound twice as good. They don't.

If budget allows and you can only pick one: **Sony 1000X The Collexion**, every time, until the PX8 S2 hits a meaningful discount.
