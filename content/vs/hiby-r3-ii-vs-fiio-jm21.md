In the sub-$200 DAP market, two products dominate the conversation: the **HiBy R3 II** at $179 and the **FiiO JM21** at $199. They look like direct competitors — both pocketable, both around the same price, both targeting the "I want a DAP but not a flagship" buyer. But the philosophies couldn't be more different.

Short version: **R3 II is a focused music player, JM21 is a streaming Android device with a great DAC**. Pick by how you actually consume music.

## Specs side by side

| | **HiBy R3 II** | **FiiO JM21** |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** | $179 | $199.99 |
| **DAC chip** | Dual ES9219C | Dual Cirrus Logic CS43198 |
| **OS** | HiByOS (dedicated firmware) | Android 13 (open) |
| **SoC** | Embedded (not user-visible) | Qualcomm Snapdragon 680 |

The headline differences: **R3 II is half the weight, has more battery life, but no streaming apps. JM21 has 2x the power, full Android, but is bigger and runs shorter.**

## Sound

### DAC implementation

- **R3 II**: Dual ES9219C — a "warm" ESS chip with slight harmonic richness. HiBy's tuning leans toward a **mildly warm, musical presentation**. Vocals are forward; bass has body; treble is smooth.
- **JM21**: Dual CS43198 — Cirrus Logic's flagship mobile DAC. Neutral, clean, **slightly warm** but more transparent than the ES9219C. Doesn't color your IEMs.

**Most listeners can't tell the two apart in a blind test.** Both are very capable at the price.

### Output power — this is the real difference

- **R3 II**: 380 mW @ 4.4 mm balanced. Enough for sensitive IEMs (anything 100+ dB sensitivity).
- **JM21**: 700 mW @ 4.4 mm balanced. Drives demanding IEMs (Letshuoer S12 Pro, low-sensitivity planars) cleanly.

If you own (or plan to own) any IEM below 100 dB sensitivity, **JM21 is the clear pick**. The R3 II will technically drive them but the bass won't develop properly.

### Black background

Both DAPs have excellent noise floors. You can plug a 120 dB Moondrop Aria 2 into either and hear no hiss.

## Streaming — the dealbreaker

This is where the two diverge hardest.

**JM21**: Full Android 13. Install Spotify, Tidal, Apple Music, Qobuz, YouTube Music, Bandcamp, Soundcloud, anything. The catch: 3 GB RAM means **don't install more than 2-3 apps**. FiiO Music + 1 streaming service is the safe combo.

**R3 II**: No app store. No Spotify, no Tidal, no Apple Music. Local files only (microSD), plus **Bluetooth receiver mode** (stream from phone over LDAC, R3 II plays). That last mode is actually useful — let your phone do the streaming, let the R3 II do the DAC work.

**The real question**: do you have a local library, or do you stream?

- **Local library (FLAC/DSD/etc.)**: Either works. R3 II is more dedicated, JM21 is more flexible.
- **Streaming primary**: JM21, no contest. R3 II via Bluetooth is workable but not ideal.

## Build and ergonomics

**R3 II**:
- **118 g, fits in a coin pocket** of jeans
- Aluminum body, premium feel
- 3.2" display — small but legible
- 4 physical buttons on the side (volume + skip + power)
- Available in Red, Silver, Black

**JM21**:
- 165 g, fits in a jeans pocket but you feel it
- Aluminum frame, glass back (fingerprint-prone)
- 4.7" display — phone-grade-ish for browsing Android
- 4 physical buttons (volume + play + skip)
- Black only

**Winner**: depends on portability priority. R3 II is genuinely ultra-portable; JM21 is "regular size".

## Battery

- **R3 II**: ~16 hours local playback
- **JM21**: ~12.5 hours local; ~8-9h streaming

R3 II wins for endurance. JM21's Android overhead and bigger screen eat power.

## Use case matrix

| Scenario | Pick | Why |
|----------|------|-----|
| Spotify / Tidal user | **JM21** | R3 II can't install apps |
| Pure local library (your own FLAC/DSD) | **Either** | Both excellent for offline |
| Drive Letshuoer S12 Pro / hard-to-drive IEMs | **JM21** | 700 mW > 380 mW |
| Drive easy IEMs (Aria 2, Hexa) | **Either** | Both have plenty of power |
| Travel light / gym | **R3 II** | 118 g + 16h battery is unbeatable |
| All-day desk use as USB DAC | **JM21** | Bigger battery while plugged + Android multitasking |
| Bluetooth-only listener (use phone as source) | **R3 II** | Cheaper, Bluetooth receiver mode is solid |
| First DAP for an audiophile | **JM21** | More flexible, future-proof |
| Backup DAP | **R3 II** | Cheaper, lighter, simpler |

## Verdict

### Pick the HiBy R3 II if...

- You have a **local music library** and don't want to mess with apps
- You want the **most portable DAP possible** (118 g, real pocket-fit)
- You drive **easy IEMs** (sensitivity 105+ dB)
- You're OK with **Bluetooth-only streaming** from your phone
- You want **max battery life**

### Pick the FiiO JM21 if...

- You **stream primarily** (Spotify/Tidal/Apple Music)
- You drive **demanding IEMs** (need 500+ mW)
- You want a **larger display** for Android navigation
- You want **USB DAC functionality** to use with a PC at desk
- You **want one device** that does it all (Android = flexibility)

### The honest take

For 80% of buyers in 2026, **the JM21 is the smarter pick** at $20 more. Android + 700 mW + comprehensive streaming = more flexible long-term. The R3 II is the right pick for a specific buyer profile: local library + ultra-portable + simple workflow.

If you're not sure, **JM21 is the safer default**.
