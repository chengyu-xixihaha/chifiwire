Tin HiFi has built its reputation on planar IEMs that punch hard technically and punish your source even harder. The **P2 Plus** was the company's previous planar flagship — a refined, neutral, beautifully built single-12mm planar that we praised as "the most refined planar IEM Tin HiFi has shipped" while warning it was one of the hardest IEMs to drive at any price. Now there's a new top dog: the **P2 Ultra**, a $599 flagship wrapped in genuine 24K gold-plated shells and built around a third-generation 12mm ultra-thin planar driver.

The obvious question for anyone who already owns — or was about to buy — the P2 Plus is whether the Ultra justifies the jump. Both are demanding planars in the same family, with the same headline driver size and the same "needs a real amp" reputation. The Ultra raises the stakes: it reportedly wants **3W or more** before it sounds like a flagship at all. This comparison breaks down where the Ultra actually moves the needle — and where the P2 Plus remains the smarter buy.

## Short version / TL;DR

- **Buy the P2 Ultra** if you have serious desktop power (3W+ on tap), want the most resolving, fastest planar Tin HiFi makes, and value the modular cable plus 24K gold-plated build as a flagship statement.
- **Buy the P2 Plus** if you want most of the planar magic for less money, run a strong portable/desktop chain (but not a 3W+ powerhouse), or simply don't want to gamble on feeding the hungrier Ultra.
- **Buy neither** if you only listen from a phone or a basic dongle — both will sound lean and lifeless. ## Specs side by side

| Spec | P2 Ultra | P2 Plus |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Price** | $599 | $620 |
| **Driver** | 3rd-gen 12mm ultra-thin planar, 0.02mm nanoscale-coated diaphragm, dual-layer N52 magnets | Single 12mm planar magnetic |
| **Impedance** | 32 Ω | 16 Ω |
| **Sensitivity** | ~98 dB (@ 1 kHz) | ~98 dB/Vrms (very low) |
| **Frequency response** | 7 Hz – 50 kHz | 10 Hz – 40 kHz |
| **Shell** | 24K heavy-gold-plated, spiral conch acoustic chamber | CNC aluminum, gold-plated finish |
| **Cable** | Flagship modular: OCC single-crystal copper + silver-plated, swappable 3.5mm / 4.4mm / USB-C | Mogami 2381, 0.78mm 2-pin, 3.5mm SE only |
| **Connector** | 2-pin (QDC-style) | 2-pin (recessed) |
| **Case** | First-layer cowhide storage case | Cardboard accessory box, Spinfit tips |

The two share a family resemblance — same 12mm planar concept, same gold-plated aesthetic, same brutal power appetite — but the Ultra is a clean generation forward on paper. The third-gen ultra-thin diaphragm, dual-layer N52 magnet arrays, wider 7 Hz–50 kHz bandwidth, and a genuinely modular flagship cable are the headline upgrades. The P2 Plus counters with its excellent Mogami 2381 cable, but that cable is fixed at 3.5mm single-ended — a real limitation if you run a balanced chain.

## Sound

Both IEMs sit in neutral-to-natural territory, but the Ultra is the more ambitious tuning.

**Bass.** The P2 Plus delivers tight, fast, reference sub-bass that extends cleanly to 20 Hz, with mid-bass deliberately restrained — accurate rather than fun. The Ultra leans slightly warmer: it has weighty, textured mid-bass that stays fast and tight, though its sub-bass rolls off a touch. If you want maximum sub-bass slam, neither is your IEM (that's a Letshuoer-style tuning), but the Ultra's mid-bass has more body and tactility.

**Mids.** This was the P2 Plus's biggest win over the original P2 — natural, well-positioned vocals with correct weight. The Ultra builds on that: midrange described as "natural yet fulfilled," with clear, highly intelligible vocals and notably lifelike positioning. Both are honest with timbre; the Ultra simply images more precisely.

**Treble.** The P2 Plus refined the original P2's brightness without losing extension — cymbals shimmer, hi-hats have texture, with a hint of upper-treble energy that can read as bright. The Ultra's treble is ultra-fast, controlled, and brilliant, extending well past 20 kHz with exceptional percussion articulation — yet reviewers call it non-fatiguing despite the extra resolution. That combination of more detail without more harshness is the Ultra's clearest sonic advantage.

**Stage and imaging.** Both throw a wide stage with strong depth, approaching open-back perception in IEM form. The Ultra edges ahead on imaging precision and overall resolution — which it should at this tier. The gap is real but incremental; this is a flagship-vs-flagship refinement, not night-and-day.

## Driveability & source pairing

This is the axis that matters most, and the one most likely to ruin your experience if you ignore it.

The **P2 Plus** is already one of the hardest-to-drive IEMs at any price. With its very low ~98 dB/Vrms sensitivity, it needs a real headphone amp — think FiiO K7 (2W balanced), Topping A50s, or SMSL SH-9 — to develop bass and dynamics. From a phone or a basic dongle it sounds thin and lifeless, and you'll swear you got a defective unit.

The **P2 Ultra is even more demanding.** Reviewers report it needs a *minimum of 3W* to scale toward its potential, with best results from sources delivering well over that (one reviewer landed on ~5.6W from a HiFiMan EF600 on high gain). Underpowered, it goes "lean and lifeless, with compressed macro and micro dynamics" — exactly the failure mode the P2 Plus warns about, only worse. Its 32 Ω impedance and modest sensitivity make moderately powered portables a non-starter for full performance.

Practical takeaway:

- **Phone / basic USB-C dongle:** inadequate for either. Do not buy.
- **Strong dongle / balanced portable (BTR7, JM21):** workable for the P2 Plus; still short of the Ultra's potential.
- **Desktop amp (FiiO K7, Topping A50s):** the floor for the P2 Plus; usable for the Ultra.
- **High-power desktop (3W+, e.g. HiFiMan EF600 class):** what the **Ultra actually wants** to sing.

If your source ceiling is a portable rig, the P2 Plus is the more sensible target and the Ultra will leave performance on the table. Check our [best ChiFi DAPs]({{< ref "guides/best-chifi-daps-2026" >}}) before committing to either — the source decision is more important here than the IEM choice.

## Build & accessories

Both are premium objects. The P2 Plus pairs a CNC-machined, mirror-finish gold-plated aluminum shell with the standout **Mogami 2381** pro-audio cable and a thoughtful accessory box (Spinfit tips, filters, tweezers, cleaning brush). It's heavier than typical IEMs, which can affect long-session comfort.

The P2 Ultra goes further on materials and flexibility: **genuine 24K heavy-gold-plated shells** with a spiral conch acoustic chamber, a **flagship modular cable** (OCC single-crystal copper + silver-plated) that swaps between 3.5mm, 4.4mm balanced, and USB-C terminations, plus a first-layer cowhide storage case. The modular cable is the meaningful functional upgrade — it natively supports the balanced sources the Ultra needs, where the P2 Plus locks you to 3.5mm single-ended out of the box.

## Who should buy which

### Buy the P2 Ultra if

- You own a high-power desktop amp (3W+) and want to hear the most resolving, fastest planar Tin HiFi makes.
- You run a balanced chain and value the swappable 3.5mm / 4.4mm / USB-C modular cable.
- You want flagship build and a slightly warmer, more textured low end with cleaner, more extended treble.
- The 24K gold-plated shell as a statement piece matters to you.

### Buy the P2 Plus if

- You want most of the planar performance for less outlay and effort.
- Your best source is a strong portable or a mid-tier desktop amp, not a 3W+ powerhouse.
- You prefer the reference-neutral tuning and don't need balanced flexibility.
- You'd rather not gamble on feeding the even hungrier Ultra.

## Verdict

The P2 Ultra is the better IEM — more resolving, faster, with cleaner treble extension and a more flexible modular cable — and at $599 it isn't even pricier than the $620 P2 Plus on paper. But "better" comes with a string attached: it demands **3W+** of clean power to deliver what you paid for. The P2 Plus is already a torture test for sources; the Ultra raises that bar higher.

So the honest recommendation hinges on your amp, not your wallet. If you have a serious desktop chain, the Ultra is the flagship to get and the P2 Plus becomes the value alternative.
