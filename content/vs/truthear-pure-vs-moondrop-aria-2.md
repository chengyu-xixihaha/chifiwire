If you've decided you want **warm, musical, lush tuning** in the sub-$100 range (rather than analytical / detail-focused like [Hexa]({{< ref "reviews/truthear-hexa-review" >}})), the two main picks are:

- [Truthear Pure]({{< ref "reviews/truthear-pure-review" >}}) ($89.99) — 1DD+3BA hybrid, Listener-tuned
- **Moondrop Aria 2** ($89) — single dynamic driver, VDSF tuned

This guide breaks down the actual sonic differences, comfort trade-offs, and use cases for each.

**Short version**: Pure is the **modern Harman-leaning warm** pick. Aria 2 is the **classic single-DD timbre** pick. Different philosophies — both valid.

## Specs side by side

| | **Truthear Pure** | **Moondrop Aria 2** |
|---|------------------|---------------------|
| **Driver config** | 1 DD + 3 BA hybrid | Single 10mm dynamic |
| **DD diaphragm** | 10mm LCP + silicone | 10mm LCP |
| **DD magnet** | N52 rubidium | N52 |
| **BAs** | 3× custom | None (single-DD) |
| **Impedance** | 18 Ω | 32 Ω |
| **Sensitivity** | 120 dB/Vrms | 122 dB/Vrms |
| **Shell** | CNC aluminum faceplate + resin | Stainless steel + brass |
| **Cable** | 0.78mm 2-pin | 0.78mm 2-pin |
| **Tuning** | Listener (Headphones.com) warm-leaning | Moondrop VDSF |
| **MSRP** | $89.99 | $89 |

The key difference: **Pure has 3 BAs adding mid/treble specialization**; Aria 2 relies on a single dynamic driver doing everything.

## Sound — by frequency

### Sub-bass

**Tie.** Both reach down well; both stay clean. Pure has slightly more sub-bass authority due to the LCP+silicone DD, but the difference is small.

### Mid-bass

**Aria 2 has more mid-bass impact.** This is the most-noticeable difference for first-time listeners. Aria 2's mid-bass is "warmer / fatter"; Pure is tighter.

For drums-heavy music (rock, hip-hop), Aria 2 hits harder. For balance, Pure is cleaner.

### Lower mids

**Aria 2 slightly fuller.** Single-DD topology benefits here — no crossover handoff. Cello, low piano, electric bass all sound a touch more natural on Aria 2.

### Mids / vocals

**Pure wins on vocal articulation; Aria 2 wins on naturalness.**

- **Pure**: 3 BAs in the upper mids deliver excellent detail and presence. Vocals are forward, clear, articulated.
- **Aria 2**: Single-DD smoother / more "real" but slightly less detailed.

For listeners new to BA-rich IEMs: Aria 2 sounds more "natural" because it doesn't have the slight plasticky BA character.
For experienced audiophiles: Pure's articulation and detail win.

### Upper mids / treble

**Pure more extended; Aria 2 smoother.**

Pure's BAs deliver real treble extension to 15+ kHz. Aria 2's single DD rolls off slightly earlier but is smoother.

For **treble-sensitive listeners**: Aria 2 is more forgiving.
For **detail-focused listeners**: Pure delivers more sparkle.

### Soundstage and imaging

- **Width**: Both similar; Pure marginally wider (BA top end)
- **Depth**: Aria 2 better (single-DD coherence creates more 3D feeling)
- **Imaging**: Pure more precise; Aria 2 more natural

This is the classic **hybrid vs single-DD** tradeoff. Hybrids excel at separation; single-DDs excel at coherence.

### Coherence

**Aria 2 wins.** One driver = no crossover. The whole frequency range sounds like it's coming from "one place."

Pure's coherence is better than most hybrids but can't match single-DD.

## Source pairing

Both work fine from phones.

**Pure** is more sensitive (120 dB) and lower impedance (18 Ω) — phone is enough.
**Aria 2** at 32 Ω needs a touch more power. Still phone-friendly, but a dongle DAC unlocks more.

If you have a **[Fosi DS2 dongle]({{< ref "guides/best-dongle-dacs-2026" >}})** or better, both shine.

| **Pop / Vocal-led** | Excellent | Very good | Pure |
| **Rock / Alternative** | Very good | Excellent | Aria 2 |
| **Hip-hop** | Good | Excellent | Aria 2 |
| **Electronic / EDM** | Good | Very good | Aria 2 (mid-bass) |
| **Jazz** | Very good | Excellent | Aria 2 (timbre) |
| **Classical** | Excellent | Very good | Pure (detail) |
| **Acoustic** | Very good | Excellent | Aria 2 |
| **Metal** | Very good | Good | Pure (detail) |

Aria 2 leans toward "musical, fun, mid-bass-rich." Pure leans toward "neutral-warm, detailed, vocal-forward."

## Build, fit, comfort

- **Pure**: lightweight resin + aluminum face. Comfortable for long sessions.
- **Aria 2**: heavier metal shell. More premium feel but slightly more ear pressure on small ears.

For small-ear users — see [small ears guide]({{< ref "guides/best-chifi-iems-for-small-ears" >}}). Pure fits more universally.

**Cable**: Both ship with workable but unremarkable cables. Both 0.78mm 2-pin standard.

## Where each falls short

### Pure weaknesses
- Slight BA "plasticky" character on some acoustic recordings
- Less mid-bass slam than Aria 2
- Multi-driver coherence isn't quite single-DD level

### Aria 2 weaknesses
- Less detail retrieval than multi-BA hybrids
- Treble extension slightly limited
- Heavier shell can be tiring

## When to pick which

### Get **Pure** if:
- You listen mostly to **vocal-led music** (pop, R&B, jazz vocalists)
- You want **modern Harman-leaning warm** tonality
- You value **detail retrieval** alongside warmth
- You have small ears (lighter shell)
- You want the **Listener tuning** specifically (Headphones.com signature)

### Get **Aria 2** if:
- You want **classic single-DD timbre** that feels "real"
- You listen mostly to **rock / hip-hop / electronic** (mid-bass matters)
- You're **treble-sensitive** (smoother top end)
- You value **coherence over articulation**
- You want the **proven multi-year recommendation** (Aria has been a default since 2022)

### Get **both** if:
- You're building a reference library
- You want to compare hybrid vs single-DD topologies in the same warm-tuning family

## The community consensus

**r/iems sentiment 2026**:
- **First IEM, vocal-focused**: Pure
- **First IEM, all-around**: Aria 2 (more forgiving)
- **Upgrade from older Aria**: Aria 2 (direct successor)
- **Coming from Hexa, want warmer**: Pure (same brand DNA)
- **Coming from Hexa, want different**: Aria 2 (different topology)

Both are excellent. The "wrong pick" is rare here — most listeners can adapt to either.

## The honest answer

For most listeners new to ChiFi: **Aria 2** is the safer first pick. Single-DD warmth is the most universally pleasant tuning.

For listeners coming from analytical IEMs who want vocals-forward warmth: **Pure** is the more targeted upgrade.

If you can demo both — do that. They're closer in price than they are in personality.
