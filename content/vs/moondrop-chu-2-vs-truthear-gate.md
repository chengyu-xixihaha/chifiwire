If you're spending $20 on IEMs in 2026, two names dominate the conversation: the **Moondrop Chu 2** ($20) and the **Truthear Gate** ($21.99). They target the same buyer — somebody upgrading from Apple EarPods or random Amazon junk to their first "real" audiophile IEM — and they take basically the same approach: single dynamic driver, Harman-derived tuning, basic accessories, killer value.

Which one to buy depends on three things: cable, signature preference, and how much you trust each brand's QC. Let's break it down.

## Specs side by side

| | **Moondrop Chu 2** | **Truthear Gate** |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** | $19.99 | $21.99 |
| **Driver** | Single 10mm dynamic, dual-cavity DD | Single 10mm dynamic, LCP diaphragm |
| **Impedance** | 28 Ω | 16 Ω |
| **Sensitivity** | 120 dB/Vrms | 120 dB/Vrms |
| **Frequency response** | 12 Hz – 25 kHz | 20 Hz – 20 kHz |
| **Cable** | **Fixed** (non-detachable) | **Detachable** 2-pin 0.78mm |
| **Cable termination** | 3.5mm SE | 3.5mm SE |
| **Shell** | Zinc alloy | Resin with metal faceplate |
| **Tuning** | Moondrop VDSF (Harman variant) | Harman-derived, slightly more sub-bass |
| **Accessories** | 6 sets silicone tips, no case | 3 sets silicone tips, fabric pouch |

The biggest spec difference: **Gate has detachable cable, Chu 2 doesn't**. Everything else is roughly equivalent.

## Sound — round by round

### Bass

- **Chu 2**: Tight, polite mid-bass, sub-bass extends but doesn't slam. Bass guitars articulate but don't rumble.
- **Gate**: More sub-bass emphasis, slightly more mid-bass body. Kicks have more weight; electronic music has more drive.

**Winner**: Gate for bass-heavy genres, Chu 2 for acoustic / vocal-forward music.

### Mids

- **Chu 2**: Slightly forward, clean, vocal-emphasized. Moondrop's VDSF tuning gives female vocals an airy quality.
- **Gate**: Mids are slightly more neutral, less forward. Vocals are clear but don't pop the way Chu 2 does.

**Winner for vocals**: Chu 2.

### Treble

- **Chu 2**: Smooth, polite, won't fatigue. Loses some sparkle on cymbals.
- **Gate**: Slightly more upper-treble energy, more shimmer on hi-hats. Some find it occasionally sibilant on bright recordings.

**Winner depends on preference**: Chu 2 for fatigue-free, Gate for more "lively" presentation.

### Soundstage and imaging

Honestly close. Both produce intimate stages with decent imaging for the price. **Slight edge to Gate** for stage width, but it's marginal.

### Technical performance

Both are class-appropriate for $20 — neither will out-resolve a $50 IEM. **Detail retrieval is comparable**; transient response is comparable. You're not getting "better sound" by picking one over the other — you're picking a flavor.

## Build, fit, accessories

### Moondrop Chu 2
- **Zinc alloy shell** — heavier, premium feel
- **Fixed cable** — if it breaks, IEM is dead
- **6 sets of silicone tips** — generous for $20
- **No case** — sleeve only
- Ergonomics: comfortable but the weight is noticeable in longer sessions

### Truthear Gate
- **Resin shell with metal faceplate** — lighter, more "plasticky" feel but comfortable
- **Detachable 2-pin cable** — replaceable if it breaks, can upgrade to balanced
- **3 sets of silicone tips** — basic
- **Fabric pouch** — slightly better than no case
- Ergonomics: very comfortable, lightweight for hours

**Winner**: Tie. Chu 2 wins build feel, Gate wins serviceability.

## The cable question — actually important

The **fixed cable on the Chu 2 is the biggest risk factor**. If you pull the cable wrong, snag it on something, or the IEM-side strain relief fails, **the whole IEM is dead**. At $20, that's "replace it" pain — not catastrophic, but annoying.

The Gate's detachable 2-pin cable means **a $5-10 replacement cable** fixes any cable issue. You can also upgrade to a balanced cable if you eventually buy a DAP with 4.4mm output.

**For longevity, the Gate has a real advantage.** This is the single biggest reason most reviewers default to recommending the Gate over the Chu 2 in 2026.

## Verdict

### Pick the Moondrop Chu 2 if...

- You want the **most refined VDSF tuning under $30** (Moondrop's house sound)
- You listen primarily to **vocals, pop, acoustic, classical**
- You prioritize **build quality** (zinc alloy vs resin)
- You **don't plan to upgrade cables** ever
- You **don't care about treble sparkle**

### Pick the Truthear Gate if...

- You want **detachable cable for longevity** (the strongest argument)
- You listen primarily to **EDM, hip-hop, electronic genres**
- You **might buy a balanced DAP** in the future
- You want **slightly more treble shimmer**
- You can tolerate **resin shell instead of metal**

### The honest take

In 2026, **most buyers should default to the Truthear Gate** for one reason: **the cable**. At $20, paying an extra $2 to insure against cable death is obvious. The sound differences are small enough that they shouldn't override that.

The **Chu 2 is the right pick if vocals are your primary use case** — Moondrop's vocal tuning is genuinely better — or if you specifically value the metal shell.
