Truthear shipped two flagship sub-$100 hybrids within 18 months. The **Hexa** ($79) defined "reference tribrid" for the budget tier. The **Pure** ($89.99) — tuned by **Listener** from Headphones.com — is the lush, warm counterpart.

They share the same shell, the same brand, and almost the same price. They sound completely different. Pick the wrong one and you'll be disappointed for reasons you can't articulate.

This guide breaks down which fits which listener.

## Specs side by side

| | **Truthear Hexa** | **Truthear Pure** |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** | $79.99 | $89.99 |
| **Driver config** | 1 DD + 3 BA (10mm LCP DD + composite BA + high BA) | 1 DD + 3 BA |
| **DD** | 10mm LCP, custom voice coil | 10mm liquid crystal dome with silicone suspension, **N52 rubidium magnet** |
| **Impedance** | 20.5 Ω | 18 Ω |
| **Sensitivity** | 120 dB/Vrms | 120 dB/Vrms |
| **Frequency response** | 8 Hz – 40 kHz | 20 Hz – 20 kHz |
| **Shell** | Resin with metallic matte faceplate | Resin body + **CNC aluminum faceplate** |
| **Tuned by** | Truthear in-house (Harman-derivative) | **Listener** (Headphones.com) |
| **Tuning target** | Neutral with slight sub-bass lift | Warm, vocal-forward, polite treble |
| **Cable** | 3.5mm SE (modular cable $25+ extra) | 3.5mm SE |

**Same shell shape**, so cables and tips swap freely. The audible difference is **entirely in the tuning** + the N52 magnet's effect on bass authority.

## Sound — round by round

### Bass

**Hexa**: Tight, fast, controlled. The 10mm LCP DD has decent sub-bass extension but the focus is precision. Kick drums punch but don't rumble.

**Pure**: Meatier mid-bass, more sub-bass authority (the N52 magnet earns its keep). Bass guitars have more weight and warmth.

**Winner by genre**:
- Rock / pop / hip-hop → **Pure**
- Classical / jazz / acoustic → **Hexa** (less bass coloration)
- EDM → **Pure** (more slam)

### Mids

**Hexa**: Clean, slightly forward, accurate. The BAs handle the mids and they're transparent. Some find it slightly cold or "studio monitor".

**Pure**: Warmer, lusher, more present. Vocals — especially male vocals — have body and chest resonance. Female vocals have warmth without losing clarity.

**Winner for vocal music**: **Pure**, decisively. For instrument timbre on classical / jazz: tossup (Hexa cleaner, Pure warmer).

### Treble

**Hexa**: Bright, extended, detailed. Hi-hats glisten; cymbals shimmer. A small percentage of users find it slightly sibilant.

**Pure**: Politer, smoother, rolled-off above 10 kHz. Cymbals are present but lack sparkle. Won't fatigue you over long sessions.

**Winner**: **Hexa for sparkle**, **Pure for fatigue-free listening**. Treble-sensitive listeners should pick Pure without hesitation.

### Soundstage and imaging

**Hexa**: Wide stage, precise imaging. The 3 BAs help separate instruments cleanly.

**Pure**: Similar width, slightly more intimate. The warmth brings vocals closer; the brighter Hexa pushes them back.

**Winner**: **Hexa edges it** for stage width. Both are good for the price.

### Technical performance

**Hexa**: Higher resolution, more detail retrieval, sharper transients. **The technical winner**.

**Pure**: Class-appropriate but slightly behind Hexa on detail. **Trades resolution for tonal warmth**.

If you A/B them on a fast classical recording (Stravinsky Firebird Suite), Hexa will reveal more micro-detail. On a vocal recording (Norah Jones, Adele), Pure will sound more "right".

### Source synergy

**Hexa**: Easy to drive (20.5 Ω + 120 dB) but **scales noticeably with better sources**. Dongle DAC → DAP improves it audibly.

**Pure**: Same sensitivity, similar scaling but slightly more forgiving of mediocre sources (the warmth covers source noise).

**Winner for "phone-only" listeners**: **Pure** (more forgiving). **Winner for upgrade-path**: **Hexa** (more headroom).

## Build, fit, accessories

**Hexa**:
- Resin shell, lightweight
- Glittery metallic faceplate (divisive but unique)
- Stock plain 4-strand cable, 3.5mm SE only
- Basic tip selection

**Pure**:
- Resin body + **CNC aluminum faceplate** (more premium look)
- Same shell shape as Hexa (comfortable for hours)
- Stock cable identical to Hexa
- Same basic tip selection

**Winner on build feel**: **Pure** (the aluminum faceplate makes it look more expensive). On accessories: tie (both ship same package).

## By genre — direct verdict

| Genre | Pick | Why |
|-------|------|-----|
| Rock / metal | **Pure** | Mid-bass weight, smoother treble for long sessions |
| Pop / K-pop / vocals | **Pure** | Warmer mids flatter voices |
| Classical / orchestral | **Hexa** | Detail and stage matter more than warmth |
| Jazz (instrumental) | **Hexa** | Brushed cymbals need sparkle |
| Jazz (vocals) | **Pure** | Vocal warmth wins |
| EDM / hip-hop | **Pure** | More sub-bass + mid-bass slam |
| Acoustic / folk | **Pure** | Natural warmth on guitars |
| Classical chamber | **Pure** | Warm strings sound right |
| Cymbal-heavy thrash metal | **Hexa** | Need treble bite |
| Gaming (casual) | **Hexa** | Soundstage helps positioning |
| Long sessions (4+ hours) | **Pure** | No treble fatigue |

## Pick the Hexa if...

- You prioritize **detail, resolution, and treble extension**
- You listen to **classical, jazz, technical/progressive music**
- You have (or plan to use) a **decent source** (dongle DAC+)
- You don't mind brighter tuning
- You want to save $10

## Pick the Pure if...

- You prioritize **warm, lush, easy listening**
- You listen primarily to **vocals, rock, pop, acoustic, R&B**
- You found Hexa slightly bright or analytical
- You want **fatigue-free 4+ hour sessions**
- You want premium-feeling build (CNC aluminum face)

## The honest take

**Most listeners who picked Hexa in 2023-2024 should consider Pure as the natural step over for genre-flexible listening**. Pure is the "you'll still love it in 6 months" pick. Hexa is the "impressive first listen, may bore you later" pick.

Audiophiles who specifically value technicality and have other warmer IEMs (like Aria 2) should stay with Hexa as their reference-leaning option.

If you're **completely new to ChiFi at this tier**, default to **Pure**.
